### Proposal Length & Scope Benchmarks

**Short Proposals (5-10 pages):**
- Single project focus
- Straightforward scope
- Fixed-price engagement
- Typical: Staff augmentation, small projects

**Standard Proposals (20-30 pages):**
- Complex projects or programs
- Multiple stakeholders
- Comprehensive methodology
- Typical: $1M-5M engagements

**Comprehensive Proposals (40-60+ pages):**
- Large programs ($5M+)
- Multi-year engagements
- Multiple competitors
- Complex team structures
- Typical: Government/public sector RFPs

**Expected Length for Current Opportunities:**
- **Terminal Island:** 25-35 pages (complex project, POLB standards)
- **CSU Systemwide:** 30-50 pages (complex multi-campus program)

### Proposal Content Elements Checklist

**Executive Summary (5-10% of proposal)**
- Client situation and needs
- CMS value proposition
- Key differentiators
- Expected outcomes
- Compelling opening

**Technical Approach (30-40% of proposal)**
- Methodology and processes
- Schedule development and management
- Cost controls approach
- QA/QC and inspection
- Reporting and communication
- Risk management strategy

**Staffing & Qualifications (20-25% of proposal)**
- Organizational chart
- Individual resumes and bios
- Relevant experience highlights
- Certifications and credentials
- Availability confirmation

**Past Project Experience (15-20% of proposal)**
- 2-3 relevant case studies
- Similar project size and scope
- Client testimonials
- Performance metrics
- Lessons applied

**Company Overview (5-10% of proposal)**
- Company background and history
- WBE/SBE/DBE certifications
- Geographic presence
- Relevant expertise
- Key differentiators

**Pricing & Commercial Terms (5-10% of proposal)**
- Fee structure
- Timeline and milestones
- Assumptions and exclusions
- Payment terms
- Optional services

### Writing Quality Standards

**Readability:**
- Clear, concise sentences (15-20 words average)
- Active voice preferred
- Outcome-focused language
- Client perspective vs. consultant perspective
- Professional but accessible tone

**Structure:**
- Visual hierarchy (headings, subheadings)
- Bullet points for lists
- Tables for comparisons
- Graphics for complex processes
- White space for readability

**Compliance:**
- Address all RFP requirements
- No placeholder text
- Complete appendices
- Required signatures
- Proper formatting per RFP specifications

### Credential Emphasis in Proposals

**Credentials to Highlight:**
1. **PSP (Project Scheduling Professional)** — Highest scheduling credential
2. **PE (Professional Engineer)** — Technical authority and licensure
3. **CCM (Claims Certified Manager)** — Specialized claims expertise
4. **PMP (Project Management Professional)** — Project management credential
5. **CCC (Certified Cost Consultant)** — Cost management specialty

**For CM Solutions Specifically:**
- Robyn Coates: PE, PSP (emphasize 20+ years + highest credentials)
- Jessica Holiday: PSP or advanced P6 certification
- Team: Mix of PE, PSP, and relevant experience

## PROPOSAL SUBMISSION BEST PRACTICES

### Pre-Submission Checklist

**Content Verification:**
- [ ] All RFP requirements addressed
- [ ] No placeholder text remaining
- [ ] Facts verified against knowledge base
- [ ] Client name and project details accurate
- [ ] Competitor analysis current and relevant
- [ ] Team bios and credentials verified
- [ ] References authorized and contacted

**Compliance Verification:**
- [ ] RFP format specifications met
- [ ] Page limits not exceeded
- [ ] Required sections included
- [ ] Appendices complete and organized
- [ ] Signature pages executed (CEO/authorized)
- [ ] Submittal method correct (email, portal, hardcopy)
- [ ] Deadline met (early submission preferred)

**Quality Verification:**
- [ ] Spelling and grammar checked
- [ ] Consistent formatting and branding
- [ ] Professional design and layout
- [ ] Graphics clear and relevant
- [ ] Executive summary stands alone
- [ ] Printing/PDF quality verified
- [ ] External review completed

**Submission Verification:**
- [ ] Receipt confirmation obtained
- [ ] Submission logged in Salesforce
- [ ] Follow-up communication prepared
- [ ] Evaluation timeline tracked
- [ ] Question response plan ready
- [ ] Interview prep scheduled (if applicable)

### Post-Submission Activities

**Immediate (Day 1):**
- Confirm receipt with client
- Thank you note sent
- Available for questions
- Internal debrief on submission

**Week 1-2:**
- Monitor for client questions
- Rapid response protocol (24 hours)
- Prepare Q&A documentation
- Update Salesforce opportunity status

**Ongoing (Until Decision):**
- Track evaluation timeline
- Identify decision drivers
- Prepare for potential interviews
- Plan negotiation strategy
- Maintain client relationship

## SUMMARY

These benchmarks reflect industry standards for successful proposals in CM Solutions' market. Key takeaways:

1. **Team composition:** Credentials (PE, PSP) and experience matter; dedicated staff preferred
2. **SBE/VSBE positioning:** Integrate as strategic advantage (CMS as prime) vs. subcontractor
3. **Technical approach:** Clear, specific methodologies demonstrate competence
4. **Proposal length & depth:** Match complexity level; comprehensive for major opportunities
5. **Quality standards:** Professional, compliant, client-focused content
6. **Competitive positioning:** Emphasize specialization, personalization, and diversity advantage
7. **Submission discipline:** Rigorous checklists and pre-submission verification

Apply these benchmarks to maximize proposal success rate and win probability on active pursuits.
