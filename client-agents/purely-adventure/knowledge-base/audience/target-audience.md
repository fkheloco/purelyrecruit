# Purely Adventure — Target Audience

## Primary Audience Segments

### Segment 1: Curious Beginners (Priority)

* **Who:** People newly interested in overlanding, boating, or adventure travel
* **Age Range:** 25-45
* **Mindset:** Excited but overwhelmed — don't know where to start
* **Needs:** Beginner guides, gear recommendations, "first trip" planning, community encouragement
* **Pain Points:** Too much scattered info online, hard to tell what's legit, intimidated by experienced communities
* **Content Preferences:** How-to guides, gear starter kits, beginner-friendly destinations, step-by-step itineraries

### Segment 2: Adventure Families

* **Who:** Parents who want to share adventure experiences with their kids
* **Age Range:** 30-50
* **Mindset:** Want safe, fun, memorable family adventures without the drama
* **Needs:** Family-friendly trip ideas, safety tips, kid-appropriate gear, moderated community
* **Pain Points:** Hard to find family-specific adventure content; most sites target solo or extreme adventurers
* **Content Preferences:** Family-friendly flagged content, destination guides with kid info, budget-friendly options

### Segment 3: Experienced Adventurers

* **Who:** Seasoned overlanders, sailors, and travelers looking for deeper content
* **Age Range:** 30-60
* **Mindset:** Know the basics, want new destinations, advanced gear reviews, and community connection
* **Needs:** Expert-level reviews, off-the-beaten-path destinations, technical deep dives, community forums
* **Pain Points:** Most content is too basic; tired of influencer-style content that lacks depth
* **Content Preferences:** In-depth gear comparisons, advanced trip reports, technical guides, user-generated stories

## Audience Psychographics

* **Values:** Freedom, exploration, self-reliance, nature, authenticity
* **Lifestyle:** Active, outdoors-oriented, willing to invest in experiences over things
* **Digital Behavior:** Researches online before trips, reads reviews, follows adventure content creators
* **Community Needs:** Positive, supportive spaces — explicitly NOT looking for toxic comment sections or Facebook drama
* **Content Consumption:** Daily news digests, gear reviews before purchases, trip planning resources

## What They're Looking For

* A **trusted hub** — one place for everything adventure, not 20 different sites
* **Personalization** — content filtered to THEIR specific interests
* **Authenticity** — real reviews, practical advice, no sponsored fluff
* **Community** — a place to geek out on their passions with like-minded people
* **Accessibility** — adventure isn't just for the wealthy or extreme — it's for everyone

## Where They Currently Go

* Overland Journal / Expedition Portal (overlanding content)
* Cruising World / Sail Magazine (boating and sailing)
* Reddit communities (r/overlanding, r/sailing, r/boating, r/adventuretravel)
* YouTube channels (gear reviews, trip vlogs)
* Facebook groups (overlanding and boating communities — but frustrated by the noise)
* Instagram (visual adventure content)

## Audience Growth Strategy

**Phase 1 (Months 1-6):** Attract beginners with SEO-optimized guides and daily digests
**Phase 2 (Months 6-12):** Build community features to retain users and encourage UGC
**Phase 3 (Year 2+):** Expand to experienced adventurer content and premium features
