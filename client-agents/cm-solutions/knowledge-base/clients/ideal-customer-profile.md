# CM Solutions - Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

## Primary Target Markets

### Market 0: PORT & MARITIME AUTHORITIES

**Characteristics:**
- Port authorities and maritime operators
- Project budgets: $50M - $500M+
- Federal and state funding common
- Complex multi-stakeholder projects
- High regulatory and environmental requirements

**Pain Points:**
- Complex schedule management (dredging, shipping coordination)
- Maritime-specific technical requirements
- Environmental and community compliance
- Federal funding compliance (Army Corps of Engineers, MARAD)
- Multi-agency coordination (USCG, EPA, local agencies)

**Buying Process:**
- RFP-driven procurement typical
- Federal compliance requirements
- Multi-stakeholder approval
- Technical qualification emphasis
- 8-12 week evaluation typical

**Value Most Appreciated:**
- Maritime/port expertise and experience
- Schedule management in constrained environment
- Environmental compliance knowledge
- Multi-stakeholder coordination
- Federal funding and compliance expertise

**Budget Expectations:**
- Federal prevailing wage rates
- Fixed-price or T&M consulting rates
- $500K - $3M+ engagements typical
- Multi-year program relationships

**Ideal Engagements:**
- Port infrastructure projects (Terminal Island example)
- Maritime facility development
- Grade separation and access projects
- Dredging and environmental coordination
- Federal-funded port improvements

**Key Examples:**
- Port of Los Angeles (POLA) projects
- Port of San Diego facilities
- Maritime terminal infrastructure
- Intermodal facility projects

- Cost control and budget discipline

**Key Examples:**

### Market 1: PRIME CONTRACTORS / GENERAL CONTRACTORS

**Characteristics:**
- Revenue: $50M - $1B+
- Project scope: Large infrastructure, industrial, or commercial projects
- Geographic focus: Western US (primary), expanding nationally
- Key need: Expert project controls and schedule management

**Pain Points:**
- Schedule overruns and cost overruns on complex projects
- Risk management and claim prevention
- Subcontractor coordination and management
- Flexibility in staffing (peak periods, specialized expertise)

**Buying Process:**
- Multiple bids collected and compared
- Director/VP of Project Management typically approves
- Proposal-driven decision process
- 2-4 week evaluation timeline typical

**Value Most Appreciated:**
- Expert-led, experienced team
- Proven track record (similar project references)
- Cost-effective vs. permanent staff
- Fast responsiveness and deployment

**Budget Expectations:**
- Project-based pricing typical ($50K - $500K per project)
- T&M hourly rates for staff augmentation
- Cost sensitivity; value-focused procurement

**Ideal Engagements:**
- Project controls support for major projects
- Staff augmentation (scheduling, cost controls)
- Dedicated Project Controls Manager role
- Multi-project programs with team needs

## CUSTOMER ACQUISITION CHANNELS

### Channel 1: RELATIONSHIP & REFERRAL

**Description:** Existing clients, partner relationships, and industry referrals
**Effectiveness:** Highest conversion rate
**Effort Required:** Relationship maintenance and networking
**Timeline:** Variable, often faster due to trust
**Responsibility:** Sales team, industry events, networking

### Channel 2: FORMAL RFP / COMPETITIVE BID

**Description:** Customers issues formal requests for proposals
**Effectiveness:** Medium conversion rate; larger budget opportunities
**Effort Required:** Significant (proposal development, coordination)
**Timeline:** 6-12 weeks typical
**Responsibility:** Proposal team, led by David Hirsch

### Channel 3: INDUSTRY CONFERENCES & EVENTS

**Description:** Networking at industry conferences, trade shows, association events
**Effectiveness:** Relationship building and thought leadership
**Effort Required:** Event presence and follow-up
**Timeline:** 2-6 month sales cycle typical
**Responsibility:** Sales team, Robyn Coates (speaking/visibility)

### Channel 4: DIRECT OUTREACH & SALES

**Description:** Outbound sales to target prospects
**Effectiveness:** Medium, requires persistence and value proposition
**Effort Required:** Sales development and relationship building
**Timeline:** 2-4 month sales cycle typical
**Responsibility:** Sales team, business development

### Channel 5: PARTNERSHIPS & ALLIANCES

**Description:** Partnerships with prime contractors, agencies, other service providers
**Effectiveness:** High (leverages partner relationships)
**Effort Required:** Partner development and management
**Timeline:** Variable, often embedded in partner sales
**Responsibility:** Executive leadership, strategic partnerships

## Disqualifying Characteristics

**NOT an Ideal Customer if:**

1. **Wrong Expertise Focus**
   - Primarily needs construction supervision (not controls)
   - Requires design expertise (not CMS focus)
   - Needs safety/compliance focus (secondary for CMS)

2. **Wrong Budget/Scale**
   - Projects <$1M (too small to justify fees)
   - Projects requiring very specialized expertise outside CMS scope
   - "Commodity" price expectations (<$50K budget)

3. **Geographic Mismatch**
   - International projects (outside CMS scope)
   - Remote locations without local presence

4. **Poor Financial Standing**
   - High-risk contractors or clients
   - Payment history concerns
   - Disputed payment history

5. **Cultural/Values Mismatch**
   - Unethical business practices
   - Unwilling to partner collaboratively
   - Unreasonable expectations or demands
