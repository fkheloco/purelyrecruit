## 25-Point Lead Qualification Scoring System

### Firmographic Fit (10 Points)
**Industry (0-5 points):**
* Construction/specialty trades: 5 points
* B2B services (engineering, staffing, etc.): 3 points
* Other industries: 0-1 points

**Revenue (0-5 points):**
* $10M-$200M: 5 points
* $5M-$10M or $200M-$500M: 3 points
* Outside range: 0 points

### Behavioral Signals (9 Points)
**Growth Indicators (0-3 points):**
* Entering new markets, significant hiring, major wins announced
* Job postings for management roles, leadership openings
* LinkedIn activity showing expansion

**Leadership Signals (0-3 points):**
* New C-suite hires or promotions
* Executive transitions or turnover
* Founder stepping back from operations

**Training/Development Signals (0-3 points):**
* Management hiring or promotions
* Development mentions in company communications
* Industry event participation, conference sponsorships

### Cultural Fit (0-6 Points)
**Four-Fires Alignment Indicators:**
* Faith-based company messaging (Christian values, faith commitment)
* Work-life balance emphasis in culture materials
* Holistic success orientation
* Values-driven leadership language
* Community involvement, corporate giving
* Long-term employee retention focus

### Qualification Tiers

| Tier | Score | Profile | Action |
|------|-------|---------|--------|
| Priority 1 (Tier 1) | 20-25 | CEO/Owner level, perfect cultural fit | Immediate outreach, Alan or Steve personal touch |
| Priority 2 (Tier 2) | 15-19 | VP/COO level, strong fit | Strategic outreach, NEPQ discovery call |
| Priority 3 (Tier 3) | 10-14 | Manager level, qualified | Email outreach, LinkedIn engagement |
| Monitor/Disqualify | <10 | Weak fit or poor cultural alignment | Track but deprioritize, disqualify if <6 |

* **Professional but authentic** - Business-appropriate yet genuine and personal

* **Consultative** - Ask questions; uncover needs; position as advisor

* **Values-driven** - Emphasis on holistic success, culture, people, purpose
* **Non-salesy** - Avoid "vendor speak"; focus on genuine partnership
* **Strategic** - Think long-term and big-picture; avoid transactional language

### When Writing Outreach
* Lead with questions and genuine curiosity
* Reference specific signals that triggered outreach
* Demonstrate understanding of their business and industry
* Emphasize Four-Fires philosophical alignment
* Position as thinking partner, not vendor
* Clear, simple, conversational language
* Brief and respectful of time

**Example Opening:** "I noticed [Company] recently hired a VP of Operations. Given your growth phase, I'd be curious about your approach to developing your leadership team—especially as you're scaling. Would you be open to a brief conversation?"

### When Writing Proposals
* Lead with their challenge and outcomes, not features
* Reference Four-Fires framework and holistic philosophy
* Emphasize culture and people development ROI
* Include construction industry examples and case studies
* Clear pricing and timeline
* Personalized to their situation
* Professional design and formatting

### When Supporting Sales Conversations
* NEPQ framework—ask strategic questions
* Uncover emotional drivers alongside business needs
* Identify cultural fit and alignment
* Build trust through understanding
* Position Four-Fires as differentiator
