## PROJECT DELIVERY METHODS

### Design-Bid-Build

**Characteristics:**
- Separate design and construction phases
- Most common delivery method
- Clear scope definition before construction

**CMS Role:**
- Cost estimation and budgeting
- Schedule development
- Contractor coordination
- Claims prevention

### Design-Build

**Characteristics:**
- Single entity responsible for design and construction
- Fast-track potential
- Integrated design-construction team

**CMS Role:**
- Program-level coordination
- Design-construction interface management
- Schedule integration
- Cost management across phases

### Construction Management at Risk (CMaR)

**Characteristics:**
- CM firm carries schedule and budget risk
- CM firm involved from design phase
- Guaranteed Maximum Price (GMP) typical

**CMS Role:**
- Schedule development and management
- Cost estimation and control
- Risk management and contingency
- Claim prevention (especially important)

### Progressive Design-Build

**Characteristics:**
- Phased approach with early start opportunities
- Design and construction overlap
- Allows construction during design

**CMS Role:**
- Phased scheduling and sequencing
- Early start logistics and coordination
- Phase-by-phase cost tracking
- Interface management

### Public-Private Partnerships (P3s)

**Characteristics:**
- Government and private entity partnership
- Long-term concession agreements
- Specialized financing and delivery

**CMS Role:**
- Program-level project controls
- Multi-phase coordination
- Long-term schedule and budget management
- Government compliance and reporting

## PROJECT REFERENCE STRATEGY

### When Developing Proposals

**Look for Similar Projects:**
1. **Industry Match:** First priority; direct industry experience
2. **Size Match:** Similar project value and complexity
3. **Delivery Method Match:** Similar project delivery approach
4. **Geographic Match:** Same region or state preferred
5. **Time Match:** Recent projects (last 5 years preferred)

**Reference Guidance:**
- Use LOSSAN for transportation projects
- Use SDG&E for energy projects
- Use water projects for water/wastewater prospects
- Always get client permission before citing as reference
- Prepare references for potential calls

### Building New References

As new projects are completed:
1. Document metrics and outcomes
2. Obtain client satisfaction feedback
3. Request reference permission
4. Build case study materials
5. Include in proposal templates
