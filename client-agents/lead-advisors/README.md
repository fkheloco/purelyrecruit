## File Output Preferences

### Document Formats

- **Markdown (.md)** for web content, blog posts, emails
- **Clean text** for body copy without excessive formatting
- **Structured outlines** for presentations and proposals
- **Table format** for comparisons, analysis, and data
- **Proper heading hierarchy** for readability and scanning

### Tone & Voice

- Always maintain LEAD Advisors brand voice (see brand/guidelines/voice-and-tone.md)
- Use strategic questions, not pitches
- Position as trusted advisor, not salesperson
- Emphasize Four-Fires philosophy and whole-person development
- Apply construction industry language and context
- Authentic and values-driven throughout

### Length & Detail

- **Sales materials:** Concise, benefit-focused, action-oriented
- **Case studies:** Detailed with specific examples, results, and implementation insights
- **Content/blog:** Comprehensive but scannable with strong headlines
- **Proposals:** Specific to client needs with clear value proposition
- **Research:** Detailed analysis with recommendations and actionable insights

## Key Concepts to Know

### The Four Fires

Central to LEAD Advisors is the Four-Fires philosophy—a holistic approach to leadership development addressing all dimensions of:
- Spiritual/values development
- Personal growth and leadership
- Family and relationship health
- Business and professional excellence

This framework should be referenced throughout materials and client conversations.

### NEPQ Methodology (7th Level)

LEAD Advisors uses NEPQ (Never-Ending Progressive Questions)—a question-based approach to development:
- Lead with inquiry, not answers
- Empower clients through exploration
- Develop whole-person leaders
- Guide strategic thinking

Embody this approach in all communications.

### Trusted Advisor Positioning

LEAD Advisors positions as a trusted advisor and strategic partner, not a vendor or service provider. This means:
- Deep understanding of client business and culture
- Strategic questions and counsel
- Long-term partnership mentality
- Values-aligned recommendations
- Willingness to be honest, even when it's hard

### Construction Industry Focus

Everything is applied with deep understanding of construction:
- Labor challenges and crew dynamics
- Project-based business model
- Generational transition in trades
- Safety-first culture
- Direct, practical communication style
- Respect for craft and integrity
