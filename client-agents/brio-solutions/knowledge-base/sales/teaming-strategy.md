# Brio Solutions - Teaming Strategy

## Teaming & Subcontractor Partnership Model

Brio Solutions operates as a specialized subcontractor and teaming partner for prime contractors bidding on large government infrastructure contracts. The business model focuses on PMIS implementation and project controls as a trusted partner for engineering firms, PMCs, and systems integrators.

---

## Strategic Rationale

### Market Opportunity
* **Larger Deal Size:** Access to $5M-$50M+ contracts vs. $200K-$500K direct consulting engagements
* **Recurring Revenue:** Multi-year PMIS support and services within prime contracts
* **Competitive Advantage:** Leverage existing PMIS expertise and aviation knowledge as differentiator
* **Growth Leverage:** Partner with 15+ prime contractors vs. direct sales to government agencies
* **Market Positioning:** Position Brio as the "PMIS and project controls specialist" for major projects

### Why This Model
* **Plays to Strength:** LA Metro PMIS experience becomes a major competitive advantage for primes
* **Capital Efficient:** Less need for business development resources; partners bring the relationships
* **Predictable Pipeline:** Multiple partnerships reduce dependency on single client wins
* **Technology Leverage:** AI-powered tools enhance partner capability and win rates

## Teaming Relationship Lifecycle

### Phase 1: Discovery & Relationship Building (Months 1-3)
**Objective:** Identify key teaming opportunities and establish executive relationships

**Activities:**
* Market research to identify target partner RFPs
* LinkedIn/network outreach to key decision-makers
* Initial conversations about teaming opportunities
* Share relevant PMIS case studies (LA Metro)
* Identify specific upcoming pursuits

**Success Metrics:**
* 15-20 introductory conversations completed
* 5-8 partners identified for specific opportunities
* 2-3 formal meetings with PM/Business Development leadership
* Teaming agreement discussions initiated

---

### Phase 2: Teaming Agreement Execution (Months 3-6)
**Objective:** Formalize partnerships with teaming/subcontractor agreements

**Activities:**
* Develop teaming proposal highlighting PMIS and PM controls expertise
* Negotiate teaming agreements with priority partners
* Establish pricing models and engagement terms
* Create standard SOW templates for PMIS implementation
* Align on certification, insurance, compliance requirements

**Success Metrics:**
* 8-12 executed teaming agreements
* Documented SOW templates by service type
* Pricing matrix established for different engagement scales
* Partner communication cadence scheduled

---

### Phase 3: RFP Pursuit & Proposal Development (Months 6-18)
**Objective:** Support partner RFP responses with Brio capabilities

**Activities:**
* Scan partner RFPs for PMIS/PM controls requirements
* Develop teaming sections for proposals
* Create PMIS project plans and implementation timelines
* Provide aviation/infrastructure expertise for technical sections
* Support pricing and resource planning

**Success Metrics:**
* 6-10 RFP responses supported with Brio as subcontractor
* 2-3 contract wins achieved
* Average proposal turnaround: 3 weeks
* PMIS/controls highlighted in key proposals

---

### Phase 4: Contract Execution & Ramp (Months 6-24)
**Objective:** Deploy PMIS and project controls services on won contracts

**Activities:**
* PMIS implementation and configuration
* Project controls team deployment
* Integration with partner's existing systems
* Staff training and knowledge transfer
* Establish reporting cadence and governance

**Success Metrics:**
* PMIS live within 60 days of contract start
* Full project controls team deployed
* 99% system uptime
* Monthly status reports delivered
* Partner satisfaction scores 8+/10

---

### Phase 5: Long-Term Partnership & Expansion (Months 12+)
**Objective:** Deepen partnership, expand into additional projects

**Activities:**
* Support for change orders and scope expansions
* Ongoing PMIS support and optimization
* Training for new team members
* Leverage success for additional teaming opportunities
* Explore adjacent services (risk management, controls, etc.)

**Success Metrics:**
* Partner retention rate 100%
* Contract extensions negotiated
* Additional projects under teaming relationship
* Upsell of additional services
* Partner referrals to other primes

## Partnership Management & Governance

### Communication Cadence
* **Monthly:** Strategic review with partner leadership
* **Bi-weekly:** Project status meetings with PM team
* **Weekly:** Operational stand-ups with embedded team
* **Quarterly:** Business review and expansion discussion

### Success Metrics Per Partnership
* Contract revenue (on track to target)
* Team utilization and billable hours
* Client satisfaction (PMIS/project controls)
* Cost performance (budget vs. actual)
* Proposal win rate (as teaming partner)

### Escalation & Issue Management
* Issues escalated by embedded Brio project manager
* Partner escalation to Brio VP Sales/Account Management
* Quarterly business reviews to address strategic concerns
* Annual partnership agreement renewal and expansion discussion

## Risk Management in Teaming Model

### Key Risks & Mitigation

**Risk:** Partner loses RFP competition
* **Mitigation:** Diversified partner portfolio (8-12 active partnerships), multiple bids per partner

**Risk:** Scope creep on PMIS implementation
* **Mitigation:** Fixed-price implementation with detailed SOW, change order process

**Risk:** Partner unhappy with results
* **Mitigation:** Clear SLAs, executive sponsorship, proactive communication

**Risk:** Team member turnover
* **Mitigation:** Cross-training, knowledge documentation, strong team culture

**Risk:** PMIS doesn't integrate with partner's systems
* **Mitigation:** Pre-contract technical assessment, vendor experience

## Competitive Advantages in This Model

1. **Scalability:** One PMIS platform can support multiple partners
2. **Recurring Revenue:** Multi-year partnerships for ongoing support
3. **Capital Efficiency:** Partners fund initial PMIS investment
4. **Market Access:** Direct access to $100M+ contracts
5. **Defensibility:** Switching costs once PMIS is embedded
6. **Cross-Selling:** Expand services into partner's other contracts
