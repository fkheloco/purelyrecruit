## Section 3: Partner Selection Criteria

### When to Use Prime vs. Subcontractor Model

#### VSCE as Prime Consultant
**Use When:**
- VSCE has lead client relationship
- VSCE team can manage overall project coordination
- Client expects single point of contact (VSCE)
- VSCE has 50%+ of the work scope
- Project is medium-sized ($500K-$2M) or smaller
- VSCE has the client relationship/knowledge advantage

**Advantages:**
- VSCE controls project direction and approach
- Client relationship belongs to VSCE
- Margin on subcontractor work
- Repeat business relationship with client

**Disadvantages:**
- Full responsibility for project delivery
- Client satisfaction directly tied to VSCE
- May require coordination of multiple subs
- Additional administrative burden

#### VSCE as Joint Venture/Co-Prime
**Use When:**
- Partner has equal or complementary capability
- Large project ($2M+) requires partnership capacity
- Partner brings critical expertise or client relationship
- Proposal requires combined capacity to be competitive
- Partner has existing client relationship or local presence

**Advantages:**
- Shared responsibility and risk
- Partner brings complementary capability
- Larger projects accessible
- Shared margins and costs

**Disadvantages:**
- Shared control and decision-making
- Partner coordination required
- Margin sharing
- Potential for disagreements on approach

**Examples:**
- Alpha CM on large CCTA on-call (Alpha provides inspection team depth)
- AECOM or WSP on very large federal projects (they provide bonding/scale)

#### VSCE as Subcontractor
**Use When:**
- Partner is prime with client relationship
- VSCE provides specialty expertise (CM, program oversight)
- VSCE supports partner's delivery capability
- Project is outside typical VSCE project type (e.g., VSCE as CM sub to design-led project)
- Partner has existing client relationship

**Advantages:**
- Lower risk (partner manages client relationship)
- Focused scope (VSCE's specialty area)
- Streamlined coordination
- Smaller administrative burden

**Disadvantages:**
- No direct client relationship
- Margin is lower
- Limited control over project approach
- Dependent on prime's management

## Section 5: Partner Engagement Process

### Phase 1: Partner Assessment & Selection (Proposal Development)

**Activity 1: Capability Match**
- Identify required services/expertise
- Review partner capabilities against needs
- Assess capacity and availability
- Evaluate cost competitiveness

**Activity 2: Client Fit Assessment**
- Confirm partner has worked with this client (if repeat agency)
- Assess partner's relationship quality with client
- Confirm no conflicts with partner's other commitments
- Review partner's track record with similar projects

**Activity 3: Partnership History Review**
- Review past collaboration with this partner
- Document strengths and any past issues
- Assess communication and responsiveness
- Confirm team compatibility

**Activity 4: Capacity Confirmation**
- Contact partner to confirm availability
- Discuss project timeline and resource requirements
- Confirm rates and cost structure
- Verify bonding and insurance capacity

---

### Phase 2: Partner Commitment & Contract (Post-Win)

**Activity 1: Engagement Agreement**
- Formal engagement letter or subcontract
- Scope definition and deliverables
- Timeline and staffing levels
- Cost structure and rates
- Payment terms and conditions

**Activity 2: Key Personnel Commitment**
- Confirm key personnel assigned to project
- Verify availability for full project duration
- Obtain personal commitment from key staff
- Document continuity expectations

**Activity 3: Work Plan Development**
- Coordinate work plan with partner
- Define interfaces and handoff points
- Establish communication protocols
- Schedule coordination meetings

**Activity 4: Insurance & Bonding**
- Obtain certificates of insurance
- Verify bonding capacity
- Confirm coverage matches project needs
- Ensure VSCE is named as additional insured

## Section 8: Partner Performance Evaluation

### Annual Partner Review Process

**Evaluation Criteria:**
1. **Quality of Work** - Did partner deliver quality work meeting standards?
2. **Team Responsiveness** - Were partner staff responsive and accessible?
3. **Budget/Cost Performance** - Did partner stay within estimated costs?
4. **Schedule Compliance** - Did partner meet schedule commitments?
5. **Client Satisfaction** - Did client appreciate partner's work?
6. **Communication** - Clear, timely communication and issue resolution?
7. **Team Compatibility** - Did partner work well with VSCE and other subs?
8. **Problem-Solving** - Did partner identify and address issues proactively?

**Rating Scale:**
- Excellent (5): Consistently exceeds expectations
- Good (4): Meets expectations and standards
- Satisfactory (3): Meets basic expectations with some issues
- Needs Improvement (2): Several areas of concern
- Unsatisfactory (1): Major issues affecting project delivery

**Action Thresholds:**
- 4.5+ Average: Continue partnership, consider for larger roles
- 3.5-4.5 Average: Continue partnership with specific feedback
- 2.5-3.5 Average: Partner discussion required; develop improvement plan
- Below 2.5: Consider alternative partners; document issues

## Section 9: Building New Partner Relationships

### Criteria for New Partner Evaluation

**Before Engaging New Partner:**
1. **Capability Gap Analysis** - Does new partner fill existing gap?
2. **Market Research** - What is reputation in market?
3. **Reference Checks** - Contact 3+ firms that have worked with partner
4. **Meet the Principal** - Assessment of leadership and culture fit
5. **Pilot Project** - Consider starting with smaller engagement
6. **Contract Terms** - Favorable rates and flexible engagement model?
