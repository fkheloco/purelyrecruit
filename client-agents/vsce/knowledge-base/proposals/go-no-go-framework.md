# VSCE Go/No-Go Evaluation Framework

## Overview
This document provides the comprehensive framework for evaluating all new RFP/RFQ opportunities. The Go/No-Go decision determines whether VSCE will pursue a specific opportunity, conditional on strategy refinement, or decline to bid.

---

## Evaluation Categories & Scoring

### Total Points: 100 (5 categories Ã— 20 points each)

### Category 1: Strategic Fit (20 points)
**Definition:** Alignment with VSCE's business strategy, market positioning, and growth objectives.

**Scoring Guidance:**

**High (16-20 points):**
- Direct alignment with core market focus (public transportation, transit agencies)
- Supports key geographic expansion (Bay Area, LA County, coastal California)
- Strengthens relationships with existing clients
- Supports stated growth targets and capabilities
- Leverages existing certifications (DBE/SBE)
- Supports team depth in strategic disciplines

**Medium (9-15 points):**
- Tangential alignment with strategic focus
- Opens new market but not priority region
- Adjacent to current capabilities
- Potential for future business development
- Moderate team impact

**Low (0-8 points):**
- Outside stated strategic focus
- Geographic mismatch with expansion plans
- Requires new capabilities not in roadmap
- One-off opportunity without pipeline potential
- Insufficient strategic value

**Examples:**
- Transit agency on-call contract (LA): High (core focus)
- Small municipality paving project (Central Valley): Low (outside region)
- Port infrastructure in SF Bay: High (strategic region, growth market)
- Parking structure in Phoenix: Low (outside geography, no growth plan)

---

### Category 2: Technical Alignment (20 points)
**Definition:** Match between RFP requirements and VSCE's technical capabilities, experience, and personnel.

**Scoring Guidance:**

**High (16-20 points):**
- Direct project type experience (3+ similar recent projects)
- Specialized expertise required (federal compliance, maritime, etc.)
- Strong team bench strength in required disciplines
- Relevant certifications and qualifications (LACMTA-approved, port-cleared, etc.)
- No capability gaps for core scope
- Personnel directly available from recent similar projects

**Medium (9-15 points):**
- Moderate experience with project type (1-2 similar projects)
- Adjacent expertise with learning curve
- Team capability with some backfill needs
- Some new certifications/approvals needed but achievable
- Minor capability gaps
- Personnel available but with transition time

**Low (0-8 points):**
- Limited or no relevant project experience
- Significant capability gaps in core areas
- Specialized expertise not in firm
- Multiple new certifications required (12+ months)
- Key personnel not available or significantly senior
- Technical risk in delivery

**Examples:**
- On-call CM for transit agency (existing similar contract): High
- Grade separation project (1 similar project, experienced PM): Medium
- Port infrastructure (no port experience, adjacent marine work): Low-Medium
- Emerging market (bike infrastructure with growing portfolio): Medium-High

---

### Category 3: Competitive Positioning (20 points)
**Definition:** VSCE's competitive advantage relative to likely competitors and market position.

**Scoring Guidance:**

**High (16-20 points):**
- Clear market advantage over competitors
- Unique qualification or experience advantage
- Existing client relationship (repeat business)
- Strong geographic/local presence advantage
- Differentiated approach or methodologies
- Prior wins in similar scope/client type

**Medium (9-15 points):**
- Competitive but not differentiated
- Strong capability but competitors have similar strength
- No existing client relationship but accessible
- Good local presence but not unique
- Moderate win probability in open competition
- Some past wins but not recent

**Low (0-8 points):**
- Highly competitive market with major competitors
- Commodity service (limited differentiation)
- No client relationship or local presence
- Competitors clearly stronger on key criteria
- First-time competing against established players
- Low historical win rate in this segment

**Examples:**
- LACMTA repeat (existing relationship, prior wins): High
- CCTA on-call (one prior project, strong local presence): High
- Port of LA (limited port experience, competing against major firms): Medium-Low
- Costa Mesa (local presence, growing bike infrastructure portfolio): Medium
- New government agency (no relationship, commodity service): Low-Medium

---

### Category 4: Risk Level (20 points)
**Definition:** Overall execution, delivery, and organizational risk. Inverse scoring: Lower risk = Higher score.

**Scoring Guidance:**

**High Score/Low Risk (16-20 points):**
- Low execution complexity
- Straightforward scope with clear deliverables
- Proven methodologies applicable
- Strong team continuity expected
- Realistic schedule and budget
- Minimal external dependencies or approvals
- Low sustainability risk (contract longer than 2 years)

**Medium Score/Medium Risk (9-15 points):**
- Moderate execution complexity
- Some new elements or processes
- Proven approaches with adaptations
- Some team transitions or new hires expected
- Tight but achievable schedule/budget
- Some external coordination required
- Moderate sustainability

**Low Score/High Risk (0-8 points):**
- High execution complexity
- Novel project type or approach required
- Limited precedent for delivery methodology
- Significant team building needed
- Aggressive schedule or tight budget
- Multiple external agencies/approvals critical
- Short-term contract with no pipeline
- Financial risk or cost overrun potential

**Examples:**
- Repeat on-call CM contract (proven delivery): High score
- New grade separation (complex, proven methodology): Medium score
- Port infrastructure with new process changes (complex coordination): Medium-Low score
- Single small project, no repeat potential (high term risk): Low score

---

### Category 5: Opportunity Quality (20 points)
**Definition:** Overall financial attractiveness, market value, and business benefit.

**Scoring Guidance:**

**High (16-20 points):**
- High revenue potential ($2M+/year for on-call or large projects)
- Strong margin potential (30%+ if known)
- Multi-year contract (3-5 years)
- Repeat/renewal potential documented
- Resource leverage (scale to multiple projects)
- Strong client credit/payment history
- Diversification benefit

**Medium (9-15 points):**
- Moderate revenue ($500K-$2M)
- Adequate margins (20-30%)
- 1-3 year terms
- Some repeat potential
- Good client credit
- Portfolio building value

**Low (0-8 points):**
- Low revenue (<$500K)
- Thin margins (<20%)
- One-time project (no renewal)
- Weak client credit or public payment history
- Significant resource commitment for return
- Portfolio value marginal

**Examples:**
- Port of LA on-call ($2M+/year, 3-year contract, repeat): High
- CCTA on-call ($1.5M-$3M/year, 3-5 years): High
- Costa Mesa project ($400K-$800K, single project): Medium
- Small municipality consulting ($150K one-time): Low

## Overall Scoring Thresholds

### 80-100 Points: STRONG GO
**Action:** Pursue aggressively
- Commit resources to quality proposal
- Develop comprehensive strategy
- Allocate senior team members
- Execute full gold team review process
- Schedule early Red Team review

**Rationale:** High strategic value, strong competitive position, acceptable risk, proven capability.

### 60-79 Points: CONDITIONAL GO
**Action:** Pursue with conditions or modifications
- Clarify scope or requirements via RFI
- Confirm team availability
- Develop specific win themes
- Consider teaming partnerships to address capability gaps
- May pursue if risk mitigation achieved
- Prioritize based on other pursuits

**Rationale:** Generally positive opportunity but requires validation or risk mitigation; consider pursuing conditionally or passing for higher-scoring opportunities.

### Below 60 Points: NO-GO
**Action:** Do not pursue
- Decline or pass on opportunity
- No proposal resources allocated
- Monitor for future iterations
- Maintain light relationship with client (if applicable)
- Consider for strategic relationship building only

**Rationale:** Significant capability gaps, risk misalignment, low competitive probability, or insufficient strategic fit; resource better allocated to higher-probability opportunities.

## Go/No-Go Evaluation Process

### Phase 1: Information Extraction (1-2 hours)
1. Download and review all RFP/RFQ documents
2. Extract key information:
   - Client and agency type
   - Scope of work and deliverables
   - Budget and contract terms
   - Schedule and submission deadline
   - Evaluation criteria
   - Teaming/subcontractor requirements
   - Required qualifications and certifications
   - Compliance and regulatory requirements

3. Identify critical evaluation questions
4. Document in Go/No-Go evaluation template

### Phase 2: RFI Development (2-4 hours)
1. Develop Request for Information (RFI) based on gaps
2. Identify knowledge sources:
   - VSCE project history and lessons learned
   - Client relationship managers
   - Teaming partner contacts
   - Public records and agency contacts
3. Submit RFI if RFP allows; otherwise conduct informal research
4. Document responses in evaluation file

### Phase 3: Knowledge Base Research (2-3 hours)
1. Search internal knowledge base for:
   - Similar project types
   - Client history with VSCE
   - Competitive intelligence
   - Team capability documentation
   - Win/loss analysis from similar opportunities
2. Document findings in evaluation report
3. Identify capability gaps or advantages

### Phase 4: Competitive Analysis (2-3 hours)
1. Identify likely competitors
2. Assess their typical approach and positioning
3. Determine VSCE advantages/disadvantages
4. Document win probability assessment
5. Identify differentiation opportunities

### Phase 5: Go/No-Go Assembly (1-2 hours)
1. Score all five categories with justification
2. Calculate total score
3. Develop recommendation (Go/Conditional Go/No-Go)
4. Outline preliminary strategy if Go decision

## Review Gates

### Red Team Review Gate
**Timing:** Pre-proposal development (if Go decision reached)
**Purpose:** Validate opportunity assessment and identify critical risks
**Participants:** Business development, operations, technical leads
**Checklist:**
- Confirm score and recommendation alignment
- Verify capability assessment accuracy
- Identify resource conflicts with other pursuits
- Validate win themes resonate with team
- Confirm teaming and personnel availability
- Address any technical concerns

### Gold Team Review Gate
**Timing:** Pre-submission (mid-proposal development)
**Purpose:** Assess proposal quality, competitiveness, and persuasiveness
**Participants:** Senior management, client leads, proposal manager
**Checklist:**
- Evaluate win theme integration throughout proposal
- Assess client focus and responsiveness to criteria
- Review visual hierarchy and presentation
- Evaluate team/personnel narrative
- Confirm differentiation from competitors
- Assess overall persuasiveness and quality

## Historical Go/No-Go Performance

### Tracking Metrics
- Pursue rate (% of opportunities evaluated that result in Go decision)
- Win rate (% of Go decisions that result in wins)
- Average score for won opportunities
- Average score for lost opportunities
- Score threshold accuracy (do 80+ scores actually win?)

### Review & Adjustment Process
- Quarterly review of Go/No-Go accuracy
- Adjust scoring criteria based on performance
- Identify systematic biases in scoring
- Update competitive assessment assumptions
- Refine risk assessment methodology

## Quick Reference: Scoring Shortcuts

**Must-Have Criteria (Automatic No-Go if not met):**
- Reasonable geographic scope (California, Western region)
- Minimum contract value ($300K+)
- Public agency or strong corporate client
- No inherent conflicts with existing clients

**Automatic Go Indicators (score likely 75+):**
- Existing client relationship (repeat business)
- On-call contract (3+ year, multi-project potential)
- High strategic fit to stated business objectives
- Available team with direct experience

**Automatic No-Go Indicators (score likely <50):**
- No relevant project experience in last 5 years
- Outside geographic service area
- Specialized expertise not in firm (12+ month development)
- Commodity service with weak competitive position
