# Pacific Northwest Infrastructure Market Intelligence

## Market Overview

The Pacific Northwest infrastructure market, anchored by Washington State and Oregon, represents a dynamic $70 billion+ investment opportunity through 2030. Driven by Sound Transit's transformative $54 billion ST3 program, federal IIJA allocations, and state climate initiatives, the PNW offers concentrated opportunity in transit-oriented development, highway modernization, and multimodal connectivity.

**Key Characteristics:** Aggressive construction timelines (2025-2033), heavy emphasis on transit equity, unionized workforce (prevailing wage +40-50% vs national average), and strong environmental/community benefits requirements.

## Sound Transit ST3 Program ($54 Billion Through 2040)

### Program Overview
- **Board-Approved Program:** $54 billion (inflation-adjusted, 2016-2040)
- **ST3 Scope:** 62 miles of new light rail, bus rapid transit, streetcar, station improvements
- **Coverage Area:** King, Pierce, Snohomish counties (Seattle metro area)
- **Implementation Timeline:** 2017-2040 (construction heavily concentrated 2023-2033)

### Major Projects & Budgets

#### West Seattle Extension
- **Total Cost:** $6.7-7.9 billion (2025-2033)
- **Scope:** 4.5 miles, 5 stations, underground alignment
- **Major Milestones:**
  - Final design: 2025 completion
  - Property acquisition: 2025-2026
  - Construction: 2026-2033
  - Revenue service: 2033
- **Key Procurements:** Design-Build contract for tunnel + stations (est. $4.5B award 2026)

#### Ballard Extension (Ballard to Downtown to UW)
- **Total Cost:** $22.6 billion (2025-2035)
- **Scope:** 9.8 miles, 10 stations, mixed underground/elevated alignment
- **Segments:**
  - Downtown to Ballard: $12.3B (2025-2033)
  - Downtown to UW: $10.3B (2025-2035)
- **Construction approach:** Multiple D-B contracts by segment, 2026-2032 award cycle
- **Workforce:** 1,200+ construction jobs average annually during peak (2027-2032)

#### Tacoma Dome Link Extension
- **Total Cost:** $4.63 billion (2025-2031)
- **Scope:** 18.5 miles, 8 new stations from SeaTac to Tacoma Dome
- **Major Milestones:**
  - EIS completion: 2025 Q2
  - Final design: 2025-2026
  - Construction: 2027-2031
  - Revenue service: 2031
- **Key Contracting:** 3-4 D-B contracts for corridor segments

#### Everett Extension (Shoreline to Everett)
- **Total Cost:** $6.8-7.7 billion (2025-2033)
- **Scope:** 9.6 miles, 6 stations, routing TBD (2025 final decision)
- **Design Status:** EIS in process (completion 2025)
- **Construction Timeline:** 2027-2033
- **Key Decision:** BRT vs light rail (expected May 2025)

#### System Expansion & Station Improvements
- **Northgate Link Station Renovation:** $400M (2025-2027)
- **Stadium Station Expansion:** $300M (2024-2026, already underway)
- **Bus Rapid Transit (BRT) Corridors:** $1.2B for 4 corridors (2025-2029)
- **First Hill Streetcar Completion:** $250M (2025-2027)

## Washington State Minority & Women's Business Enterprise (OMWBE) Certification

### Program Overview
- **Agency:** Washington State Department of Commerce
- **Certification Types:** Minority-Owned Business Enterprise (MBE), Women-Owned Business Enterprise (WBE)
- **Combined: OMWBE (Minority/Women owned)

### Eligibility Requirements
- **Gross Receipts Cap:** $31.84 million (3-year average)
- **Personal Net Worth Cap:** No cap specified, but must demonstrate commitment to business
- **Ownership Requirement:** 51% ownership by minority/women entrepreneurs
- **Management & Control:** Active, full-time involvement in daily operations
- **Proof of Status:** Minority group membership documentation required

### Certification Process
- **Application Portal:** commerce.wa.gov/omwbe
- **Processing Timeline:** 60-90 days standard
- **Documentation Required:**
  - Business tax ID and registration
  - Financial statements (3 years)
  - Proof of ownership/control
  - Business plan and tax returns
  - Certification for other states/programs (if applicable)

### Reciprocal Acceptance
- **Oregon COBID:** Not automatically reciprocal; must reapply in Oregon
- **Federal DBE:** OMWBE recognized as qualifying documentation for DBE status
- **Other States:** Individual state applications required

### WA State Certification Benefits
- **Preference in State Procurement:** 10% price preference available on some contracts
- **Bonding Assistance:** Surety bond assistance programs available
- **Small Business Resources:** Business counseling, training, networking

**Contact:** WA Commerce OMWBE, (360) 725-4000, OMWBE@commerce.wa.gov

## Disadvantaged Business Enterprise (DBE) Requirements - PNW

### Federal DBE Program (USDOT)
- **Applicability:** All federally funded transportation projects
- **Certifier:** USDOT Office of Small & Disadvantaged Business Utilization
- **Gross Receipts Cap:** $31.84 million (3-year average)
- **Net Worth Cap:** $7.5 million (excluding principal residence)
- **Ownership:** 51% minority/women/disabled veteran
- **Certification Portal:** mopcheck.dot.gov

### Oregon Certified Disadvantaged Business (COBID)
- **Administering Agency:** Oregon Department of Administrative Services
- **Gross Receipts Cap:** $23.98 million (3-year average)
- **Net Worth Cap:** $6.5 million (excluding primary residence)
- **Certification Portal:** oregon.gov/das/Business
- **Reciprocity with WA:** Not automatic; must apply separately

### Regional DBE Participation Goals
- **Federal Baseline:** 10% (USDOT requirement)
- **Sound Transit:** 15-26% by contract
- **WSDOT:** 15-20% by contract type
- **Ports:** 15-18% by contract
- **Interstate Bridge Replacement:** 15% federal + Oregon 20% MBE/WBE goal
- **King County Metro:** 12-15% by contract

## Apprenticeship Requirements & Workforce Standards

### Prevailing Wage & Union Partnerships
- **Prevailing Wage Rates:** WSDOT rates approximately 40-50% above national average
- **Rate Updates:** Quarterly adjustments
- **Classification Examples:**
  - Operating Engineer (heavy equipment): $58-65/hour + fringes
  - Laborer (general): $42-48/hour + fringes
  - Carpenter: $48-55/hour + fringes
  - Ironworker: $55-62/hour + fringes

### Apprenticeship Utilization Goals
- **Sound Transit:** 15% apprenticeship hours (15% = 150 hours per $1M contract)
- **WSDOT:** 15% on projects >$2M
- **Interstate Bridge:** 15% apprenticeship requirement
- **King County Metro:** 12-15%

### Registered Apprenticeship Programs
- **IUOE Local 665:** Operating Engineers (Seattle area)
- **Laborers Local 252, 483, 737:** Regional laborers unions
- **Carpenters Local 86, 1184:** Pacific Northwest carpenter apprenticeships
- **Ironworkers Local 86:** Structural and reinforcing ironworkers
- **Electrical Workers Local 77, 112, 125:** Electrician apprenticeships

### Program Characteristics
- **Apprenticeship Duration:** 4-5 years typically
- **Ratio:** 1 apprentice per 5-8 journeyworkers on large projects
- **Wages:** Start 50% of journeyworker rate, increase by 10% every 6 months
- **Training Hours:** 5,000-8,000 hours classroom + OJT over 4 years

**Contact:** Washington State Labor & Industries Apprenticeship Program, (360) 902-5500

## Market Conditions & Competitive Analysis

### Current Market Dynamics
- **Labor Tightness:** Prevailing wage projects compete at premium (40-50% above national)
- **Union Penetration:** 85-95% of heavy civil construction work is unionized
- **Bonding Capacity:** National bonding firms have exposure; regional/mid-market can struggle
- **Material Costs:** Stable but commodity-dependent; concrete +2-4% YoY, steel stable
- **Supply Chain:** Moderate disruption; ferries/specialized equipment may face 18-24 month leads
- **Permitting:** Moderate timeline (60-90 days); environmental review primary constraint

### Strategic Market Positioning for VSCE
1. **Sound Transit Design MATOC:** September 2025 awards - position for design services roles
2. **PM/CM MATOC:** November 2025 awards - construction management/controls opportunity
3. **Interstate Bridge:** 2026 D-B contract award - large regional opportunity
4. **Ferries Electrification:** 2025-2032 infrastructure procurement - specialized opportunity
5. **High-Speed Rail:** 2027-2028 design phase - long-term pipeline opportunity
6. **Workforce Development:** Apprenticeship integration gaining competitive advantage
