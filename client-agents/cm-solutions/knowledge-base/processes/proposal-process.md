This document outlines CM Solutions' proposal development process, including workflow, responsibilities, quality standards, and AI automation initiatives with Purely Works.

## PROPOSAL WORKFLOW

### Phase 1: OPPORTUNITY IDENTIFICATION & QUALIFICATION

**Trigger:** New business opportunity (RFP, direct request, or partnership)

**Key Activities:**
1. **Opportunity Assessment**
   - Client/project fit evaluation
   - Service fit analysis (does CMS have relevant expertise?)
   - Resource availability check
   - Timeline feasibility

2. **Win/No-Win Decision**
   - Is this worth pursuing?
   - Do we have competitive advantage?
   - Can we resource it?
   - Is proposal effort justified?

3. **Kickoff & Information Gathering**
   - Understand client requirements
   - Identify evaluation criteria
   - Clarify scope and deliverables
   - Determine pricing approach

**Responsible Parties:**
- Sales lead (business development)
- David Hirsch (Proposal Manager) — approval on complex proposals
- Robyn Coates (CEO) — strategic opportunities

**Timeline:** 1-2 weeks typical

**Output:** Proposal kickoff plan with scope, timeline, pricing approach

---

### Phase 2: PLANNING & POSITIONING

**Key Activities:**

1. **Competitive Positioning**
   - Understand competitors
   - Identify CMS differentiators
   - Develop winning strategy
   - Position strengths against client needs

2. **Team & Expertise Assembly**
   - Identify technical experts needed
   - Assign proposal writing responsibilities
   - Confirm resource availability
   - Plan internal coordination

3. **Reference & Past Project Review**
   - Identify similar projects in portfolio
   - Obtain client permission for references
   - Prepare case study/success story materials
   - Gather metrics and outcomes data

4. **Proposal Strategy**
   - Outline proposal structure
   - Identify key themes and messages
   - Plan graphics, visuals, and format
   - Estimate proposal effort and timeline

**Responsible Parties:**
- David Hirsch (Proposal Manager) — overall coordination
- Sales lead — competitive intelligence
- Technical experts — content development

**Timeline:** 1 week typical

**Output:** Proposal strategy document, team assignments, timeline

---

### Phase 3: CONTENT DEVELOPMENT

**Key Activities:**

1. **Team Assembly & Kickoff**
   - Team meeting to review strategy
   - Role and responsibility clarification
   - Timeline and milestone review
   - Template and format review

2. **Executive Summary Development**
   - Client situation overview
   - CMS value proposition
   - Key differentiators
   - Expected outcomes

3. **Technical Approach Development**
   - Methodology description
   - Process flowcharts
   - Staffing plan and resumes
   - Schedule and timeline
   - Deliverables and reporting structure

4. **Team Bios & Qualifications**
   - Robyn Coates biography (PE, PSP, expertise)
   - Key team member resumes
   - Relevant certifications (PSP, PE, etc.)
   - Project experience highlights

5. **Case Studies & Past Performance**
   - 2-3 relevant past projects
   - Metrics showing success (schedule performance, cost control, etc.)
   - Client testimonials if available
   - Lessons learned and lessons applied

6. **Pricing & Commercial Terms**
   - Fee structure and rates
   - Phasing and milestone structure
   - Assumptions and exclusions
   - Payment terms and conditions

7. **Company Overview & Differentiators**
   - CM Solutions overview
   - WBE/SBE/DBE certifications
   - Team expertise and experience
   - Thought leadership (Robyn Coates)

**Responsible Parties:**
- Technical experts — content development
- David Hirsch — coordination and review
- Robyn Coates — executive approval on major proposals
- Kristen Badar — contract language and terms review

**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks typical (depends on proposal complexity)

**Output:** Draft proposal sections from all team members

---

### Phase 4: REVIEW, EDITING, & REFINEMENT

**Key Activities:**

1. **Internal Review Cycle**
   - David Hirsch reviews for compliance and messaging
   - Technical review by relevant experts
   - Kristen Badar reviews contract language
   - Client/industry fit assessment

2. **Editing & Consistency**
   - Writing quality and clarity
   - Consistency across sections
   - Brand compliance (tagline, tone, format)
   - Alignment with strategy

3. **Graphics & Visual Design**
   - Organization charts
   - Process flowcharts
   - Schedule/timeline visuals
   - Data charts and performance metrics

4. **Quality Assurance**
   - Spelling and grammar check
   - Fact verification
   - Reference verification
   - Format and layout consistency

**Responsible Parties:**
- David Hirsch — quality control and final approval
- Editors/writers — copyediting
- Design resources — graphics and layout
- Technical experts — technical accuracy

**Timeline:** 1-2 weeks typical

**Output:** Draft proposal ready for client review/submission

---

### Phase 5: FINAL REVIEW & SUBMISSION

**Key Activities:**

1. **Executive Review**
   - Robyn Coates review (large/strategic proposals)
   - David Hirsch final approval
   - Budget/pricing review and approval

2. **Formatting & Production**
   - Final layout and design
   - Cover page and branding
   - Page numbering and table of contents
   - Executive summary finaliz ation

3. **Pre-Submission Compliance Check**
   - RFP requirement checklist verification
   - Submittal format verification
   - Required documents included
   - Signature pages completed (if applicable)

4. **Submission**
   - Delivery method (email, portal, hardcopy)
   - Confirmation of receipt
   - Submission log/tracking

**Responsible Parties:**
- David Hirsch — overall approval
- Robyn Coates — executive approval and signature
- Administrative support — production and submission

**Timeline:** 3-5 days typical

**Output:** Final proposal submitted to client

   - Contact for questions or clarification

2. **Questions & Clarifications**
   - Monitor for client questions

   - Identify key decision drivers
   - Prepare for potential negotiations

4. **Interviews/Presentations**
   - Prepare if interview/presentation requested
   - Robyn Coates involvement for strategic presentations
   - Technical team availability for Q&A
   - Follow-up after meetings

5. **Negotiation (If Selected)**
   - Pricing negotiation support
   - Contract terms review (Kristen Badar)
   - Service scope clarification
   - Agreement finalization

**Responsible Parties:**
- Sales lead — client relationship management
- David Hirsch — proposal and scope clarification
- Kristen Badar — contract negotiation
- Robyn Coates — strategic clients or presentations

**Timeline:** Ongoing until decision

**Output:** Contract executed or feedback on loss

## GO/NO-GO EVALUATION FRAMEWORK

### Purpose

Structured framework for evaluating business opportunities before committing significant proposal resources. Helps prioritize efforts, allocate team resources effectively, and improve win probability through selective pursuit.

### Evaluation Criteria

**Six Primary Factors (0-20 points each):**

1. **Market Opportunity (0-20 points)**
   - Market size and value
   - Growth potential and recurring revenue
   - Program duration and stability
   - Strategic fit with CMS growth targets

2. **Competitive Position (0-20 points)**
   - CMS competitive advantages
   - Competitor strength and incumbency
   - Differentiation opportunities
   - Win probability assessment

3. **Resource Availability (0-20 points)**
   - Team capacity and availability
   - Key resource constraints
   - Staffing requirements
   - Conflict with existing commitments

4. **Strategic Fit (0-20 points)**
   - Alignment with CMS core expertise
   - Industry and project type fit
   - Long-term relationship potential
   - Brand and portfolio alignment

5. **Financial Viability (0-20 points)**
   - Profit margin potential
   - Engagement value and size
   - Cash flow timing
   - Cost of pursuing opportunity

6. **Risk Assessment (0-20 points)**
   - Client stability and reputation
   - Scope clarity and definition
   - Timeline feasibility
   - Legal/compliance complexity

### Score Interpretation

| Score Range | Recommendation | Action |
|-------------|---|---|
| 85-100 | **STRONG GO** | Pursue aggressively; commit full resources |
| 70-84 | **GO** | Recommend pursuit with careful evaluation |
| 50-69 | **HOLD/EVALUATE** | More information needed; conditional pursuit |
| <50 | **NO-GO** | Recommend passing; focus resources elsewhere |

## PRICING APPROACHES

### Approach 1: TIME & MATERIALS (T&M)

**When Used:**
- Undefined scope or requirements
- Client comfortable with variable costs
- Flexible scope projects
- Staff augmentation engagements

**Rate Structure:**
- Hourly rates by staff level
- Robyn Coates: $250-350/hour (PE, PSP authority)
- Senior Consultants: $200-250/hour
- Consultants: $150-200/hour
- Coordinators/Analysts: $100-150/hour

**Advantages:**
- Flexibility for evolving scope
- Covers unpredictable work
- Simple contracting
- Risk mitigation for CMS

**Disadvantages:**
- Client budget uncertainty
- May not be competitive on fixed-scope bids
- Requires tracking and transparency

### Approach 2: FIXED PRICE / FIXED FEE

**When Used:**
- Clearly defined scope and deliverables
- Single project engagement
- RFP responses with defined requirements
- Competitive bidding situations

**Pricing Structure:**
- Estimated effort × hourly rates = base fee
- + overhead allocation
- + margin
- = fixed project fee

**Advantages:**
- Client budget certainty
- Competitive on proposals
- Clear scope definition
- Incentive for efficiency

**Disadvantages:**
- Risk if scope expands
- Requires detailed scope definition
- Less flexibility on requirements

### Approach 3: RETAINER / MONTHLY SERVICES

**When Used:**
- Ongoing project controls support
- Long-term engagements
- Executive advisor arrangements
- Retained expert relationships

**Structure:**
- Monthly fee for defined services
- Defined availability and hours
- Specific deliverables/reporting
- Annual commitment typical

**Advantages:**
- Predictable revenue
- Long-term client relationship
- Specialized expert availability
- Client convenience

**Disadvantages:**
- Budget risk if services exceed allocation
- Requires clear scope definition
- Less flexibility

### Approach 4: PROGRAM-LEVEL PRICING

**When Used:**
- Multiple projects within program
- Multi-year engagements
- Volume discounts possible
- Master service agreement relationships

**Structure:**
- Per-project fees with volume discounts
- Monthly retainer + project fees
- Tiered pricing by project size
- Annual budget commitment

**Advantages:**
- Long-term relationship value
- Competitive pricing at scale
- Relationship stability
- Expanded services opportunity

**Disadvantages:**
- Requires multi-year commitment
- More complex administration
- Less flexibility on individual projects
