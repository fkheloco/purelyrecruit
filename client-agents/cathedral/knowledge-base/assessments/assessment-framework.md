# Cathedral Center Assessment Framework

## Overview

Cathedral Center's assessment framework is a three-tier system designed to progressively deepen understanding of financial advisor compatibility across multiple dimensions. The framework moves from automated data collection to comprehensive expert evaluation, enabling evidence-based decision-making for M&A, recruiting, and partnership evaluation.

---

## Three-Tier Assessment System

### Tier 1: Public Data Enrichment (Automated)

**Purpose:** Rapid initial screening using publicly available information

**Data Sources:**
- SEC ADV filings (advisor registration)
- State regulatory filings
- Public business records
- Technology and systems analysis (via web presence)
- Social media and online presence
- Industry databases

**Key Metrics Captured:**
- Assets Under Management (AUM)
- Number of advisors on staff
- Regulatory compliance status
- Technology infrastructure assessment
- Service offerings
- Geographic presence
- Years in operation
- Founding date and leadership tenure

**Scoring:** Automated compatibility scoring based on public signal patterns

**Output:**
- Automated Data Profile
- Preliminary Compatibility Score (pre-DNA assessment)
- Flagged Areas of Interest
- Recommendation to proceed to Tier 2

**Timeline:** 24-48 hours (fully automated, no advisor involvement required)

**Use Cases:**
- Initial screening of M&A opportunities
- Prospect identification and qualification
- Baseline compatibility check
- Advisor network analysis

### Tier 2: Structured Questionnaires (Self-Administered)

**Purpose:** Detailed self-assessment capturing operational, strategic, behavioral, and cultural dimensions

**Time to Complete:** 30-45 minutes for average advisor

**Question Count:** Approximately 91 total questions across four modules

#### Module 1: Operations (~30+ inputs)

Deep assessment of firm operational infrastructure and systems.

**Topics Covered:**
1. **Team Structure & Staffing**
   - Number and roles of advisors
   - Support staff (operations, compliance, administrative)
   - Hiring and recruitment philosophy
   - Compensation and incentive structures
   - Key person dependencies
   - Succession planning
   - Team stability and turnover

2. **Technology & Systems**
   - Portfolio management systems
   - CRM and client management systems
   - Document management
   - Communication tools
   - Accounting and operations software
   - Cybersecurity measures
   - System integration and data sharing
   - Technology upgrade plans

3. **Compliance & Operations**
   - Compliance procedures and oversight
   - Regulatory examination history
   - Document retention and management
   - Client agreement templates
   - Fee schedules and billing processes
   - Client onboarding procedures
   - Service delivery standards
   - Performance measurement systems

4. **Growth Processes**
   - Client acquisition process
   - Marketing approach
   - Partnership and referral strategies
   - Service expansion plans
   - Geographic expansion strategy
   - Market segment focus
   - Growth capacity and constraints

5. **Client Service Model**
   - Target client type (AUM, demographics, complexity)
   - Service delivery model (team-based, advisor-centric)
   - Client communication frequency
   - Minimum account sizes
   - Service levels by client tier
   - Fee structure (flat, AUM, hourly, hybrid)

6. **Financial Management**
   - Profitability and margins
   - Cost structure
   - Cash flow management
   - Financial planning and forecasting
   - Capital requirements
   - Debt or credit obligations

#### Module 3: Behaviors (DAAN Framework)

Assessment of behavioral patterns using the DAAN behavioral typing system.

**The DAAN Model:**

DAAN is a behavioral profiling system identifying four primary behavioral patterns. Most individuals and firms exhibit combinations of these patterns.

**D - Driver**
- **Characteristics:** Competitive, results-oriented, direct, action-focused
- **Motivations:** Achievement, winning, control, quick decisions
- **Work Style:** Fast-paced, bottom-line focused, impatient with process
- **Communication:** Direct, concise, focused on outcomes
- **Leadership:** Directive, goal-focused, decisive
- **Strengths:** Speed, decisiveness, competitive drive
- **Challenges:** May skip important details, impatient with others, overly competitive

**A (1st A) - Analytical**
- **Characteristics:** Systematic, detail-focused, risk-aware, methodical
- **Motivations:** Accuracy, thoroughness, logical consistency, risk mitigation
- **Work Style:** Deliberate, process-focused, comprehensive planning
- **Communication:** Data-driven, precise, thorough
- **Leadership:** Structured, process-oriented, consensus-building
- **Strengths:** Thoroughness, quality focus, risk management
- **Challenges:** Can be slow, analysis paralysis, overly risk-averse

**A (2nd A) - Amiable**
- **Characteristics:** Relationship-focused, collaborative, supportive, people-oriented
- **Motivations:** Harmony, relationships, team success, helping others
- **Work Style:** Collaborative, consensual, relationship-building
- **Communication:** Warm, empathetic, listening-focused
- **Leadership:** Participative, supportive, team-focused
- **Strengths:** Team building, relationship management, collaboration
- **Challenges:** May avoid necessary conflict, slower decisions, overly accommodating

**E - Expressive**
- **Characteristics:** Visionary, big-picture, influence-focused, enthusiastic
- **Motivations:** Vision, impact, recognition, creative expression
- **Work Style:** Big-picture, inspirational, networking and relationship building
- **Communication:** Visionary, persuasive, story-telling
- **Leadership:** Inspirational, visionary, culture-building
- **Strengths:** Vision articulation, team inspiration, big-picture thinking
- **Challenges:** May lack operational detail, unfocused, inconsistent execution

**Assessment Approach:**
Questions identify behavioral patterns across multiple dimensions, creating a behavioral profile and compatibility assessment with target firms.

## DNA Scoring System

### Overview

Cathedral Center's DNA scoring system assesses compatibility across five core dimensions, moving beyond financial metrics to evaluate true partnership fit.

**For comprehensive scoring methodology details including quantitative dimensions, qualitative dimensions, and scoring calculations, see:** `/knowledge-base/assessments/scoring-methodology.md`

### The Five DNA Dimensions

#### 1. Culture Alignment

**Definition:** Do their daily work environment, organizational values, and operational style match your firm's culture?

**Assessment Basis:**
- Firm culture questionnaire responses
- Leadership philosophy and management approach
- Work environment and collaboration style
- Advisor autonomy and decision-making
- Internal processes and procedures
- Communication and transparency

**Scoring Methodology:**
- Compare target firm culture responses to acquiring firm culture
- Evaluate compatibility of work styles and values
- Assess potential culture clash areas
- Identify culture bridge opportunities

**Score Interpretation:**
- **90-100 (Strong Alignment):** Culture and values are highly compatible; minimal adaptation needed
- **75-89 (Good Alignment):** Generally compatible with some minor adjustments in work style or values priority
- **60-74 (Moderate Alignment):** Compatible with meaningful cultural adjustments needed; integration will require intentional culture bridging
- **40-59 (Weak Alignment):** Significant cultural differences; substantial integration effort required; risk of advisor departure
- **Below 40 (Poor Alignment):** Fundamental cultural incompatibility; high risk of partnership failure

**Integration Implications:**
- High Alignment: Relatively straightforward integration; maintain target firm's culture
- Moderate Alignment: Identify specific bridge areas; develop integration plans for value and culture gaps
- Low Alignment: Substantial cultural due diligence; may require compromises or reconsideration of partnership

---

#### 2. Values Compatibility

**Definition:** Do their core business values, ethical standards, and decision-making principles align with yours?

**Assessment Basis:**
- Stated values and ethical principles
- Regulatory compliance history
- Client relationship philosophy
- Long-term vs. short-term orientation
- Sustainability and social responsibility
- Decision-making approach and integrity

**Scoring Methodology:**
- Compare core value statements and priorities
- Evaluate regulatory and ethical track record
- Assess alignment on client relationships and service philosophy
- Evaluate long-term partnership potential

**Score Interpretation:**
- **90-100 (Strong Compatibility):** Values are deeply aligned; no fundamental conflicts
- **75-89 (Good Compatibility):** Values are generally aligned with minor philosophical differences
- **60-74 (Moderate Compatibility):** Compatible with some value differences; bridgeable through dialogue
- **40-59 (Weak Compatibility):** Significant value differences; misalignment on key principles
- **Below 40 (Poor Compatibility):** Fundamental value conflicts; ethical or compliance risks

**Integration Implications:**
- High Compatibility: Values can drive partnership; shared vision likely
- Moderate Compatibility: Identify specific value differences; clarify non-negotiables
- Low Compatibility: Substantial due diligence on specific value differences; may indicate partnership risk

---

#### 3. Behavioral Fit

**Definition:** Are their behavioral patterns (DAAN types) complementary to your team's behavioral composition?

**Assessment Basis:**
- DAAN behavioral profiles (individual and firm-level)
- Leadership behavioral style
- Team composition and behavioral diversity
- Decision-making speed and style
- Risk tolerance and innovation orientation

**Scoring Methodology:**
- Map target firm's behavioral profile (D, A, A, E composition)
- Compare to acquiring firm's existing behavioral composition
- Assess complementarity and potential conflict areas
- Evaluate whether behaviors are additive or conflicting

**Score Interpretation:**
- **90-100 (Strong Fit):** Behavioral composition is complementary; natural integration and collaboration likely
- **75-89 (Good Fit):** Generally complementary with some behavioral differences that can be managed
- **60-74 (Moderate Fit):** Some behavioral compatibility gaps; management and integration focus needed
- **40-59 (Weak Fit):** Significant behavioral differences; potential for conflict and misalignment
- **Below 40 (Poor Fit):** Fundamental behavioral incompatibility; high risk of interpersonal conflict

**Integration Implications:**
- High Fit: Team integration likely smooth; behavioral diversity is additive
- Moderate Fit: Identify behavioral differences; develop integration plans for decision-making and collaboration
- Low Fit: Substantial focus needed on behavioral integration; may require structured onboarding and team development

---

#### 4. Needs Matching

**Definition:** Do they have the specific needs that your firm is positioned to fulfill?

**Assessment Basis:**
- Operational capability gaps and challenges
- Growth constraints and limiting factors
- Resource and infrastructure needs
- Technology and system requirements
- Team and talent development needs
- Market access and opportunity needs

**Scoring Methodology:**
- Identify target firm's primary business needs
- Assess your firm's ability to fill those needs
- Evaluate degree of need/fit alignment
- Identify mutual benefit opportunities

**Score Interpretation:**
- **90-100 (Strong Match):** Target firm has critical needs that your firm excellently addresses
- **75-89 (Good Match):** Target firm has significant needs that your firm can address
- **60-74 (Moderate Match):** Some need alignment; your firm can help in certain areas
- **40-59 (Weak Match):** Limited need alignment; modest value added by partnership
- **Below 40 (Poor Match):** Target firm doesn't have significant needs that your firm addresses

**Integration Implications:**
- High Match: Clear value proposition and partnership motivation; mutual benefit evident
- Moderate Match: Value proposition exists but limited to specific areas; partial integration may be optimal
- Low Match: Limited value creation; partnership risk and motivation questions

---

#### 5. Desires Alignment

**Definition:** Do they want the same trajectory, autonomy level, outcomes, and future vision as your firm offers?

**Assessment Basis:**
- Long-term vision and growth aspirations
- Autonomy and decision-making authority preferences
- Compensation and incentive structure preferences
- Generational and succession planning
- Exit or liquidity timeline
- Geographic expansion or consolidation desires

**Scoring Methodology:**
- Identify target firm's desired outcomes and trajectory
- Compare to what your firm offers
- Assess degree of alignment on future direction
- Evaluate satisfaction with your firm's path vs. their desires

**Score Interpretation:**
- **90-100 (Strong Alignment):** Target firm desires align with what you offer; clear motivation for partnership
- **75-89 (Good Alignment):** Generally aligned with some minor differences in trajectory or timeline
- **60-74 (Moderate Alignment):** Aligned on some dimensions but with meaningful differences on others
- **40-59 (Weak Alignment):** Limited alignment on desired outcomes; divergent aspirations
- **Below 40 (Poor Alignment):** Fundamentally different desired trajectories; partnership motivation unclear

**Integration Implications:**
- High Alignment: Target firm's aspirations align with partnership opportunity; retention likely
- Moderate Alignment: Clarify expectations; ensure alignment on key trajectory decisions
- Low Alignment: Significant work needed to clarify and bridge expectations; advisor retention risk

---

### Overall DNA Score

**Calculation:**
DNA Score = Average of the five dimension scores (Culture, Values, Behavior, Needs, Desires)

**Overall Score Interpretation:**
- **90-100 (Excellent Fit):** Highly compatible partnership with strong alignment across all dimensions
- **75-89 (Good Fit):** Compatible partnership with minor areas for integration focus
- **60-74 (Moderate Fit):** Viable partnership requiring intentional integration planning and management
- **40-59 (Poor Fit):** Significant compatibility challenges; partnership requires major integration effort and carries elevated risk
- **Below 40 (Not Recommended):** Fundamental incompatibility; partnership likely to fail or require complete restructuring

## SWOT Analysis & Integration Planning

### SWOT Deliverable

Cathedral Center generates a customized SWOT (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats) analysis after compatibility calculation:

**Strengths:** Areas of high compatibility and alignment leverageable for partnership success

**Weaknesses:** Areas of significant gap or misalignment requiring mitigation or active management

**Opportunities:** Potential value creation areas and expansion/synergy potential

**Threats:** Risk factors, integration challenges, and possible failure points

### Integration Plan & Guidance

Based on SWOT analysis, Cathedral Center provides:

- **Strength Leverage Strategy** — How to maximize partnership strengths
- **Weakness Mitigation Plan** — Specific actions to address identified gaps
- **Opportunity Prioritization** — Which opportunities to pursue first
- **Threat Mitigation Strategy** — Risk reduction and contingency planning
- **Integration Timeline** — Phased approach for successful integration
- **Success Metrics** — KPIs and measurement approach

## Additional Discovery Questions

Beyond the structured assessment, Cathedral Center employs open-ended discovery questions to deepen understanding:

### Strategic Vision Questions

1. **"Would you like more Time/Impact/Money?"**
   - Clarifies primary motivation and satisfaction drivers
   - Informs partnership value proposition

2. **"Describe your perfect Practice/Firm..."**
   - Captures aspirational vision and culture
   - Reveals desires and long-term trajectory
   - Identifies gap between current state and desired future

3. **"If you could change one thing about your firm, what would it be?"**
   - Identifies primary pain point or frustration
   - Reveals growth opportunity or capability gap
   - Highlights needs assessment

### Pain & Performance Questions

4. **"Where is the Pain?"**
   - Uncovers specific operational or cultural challenges
   - Identifies integration risk areas
   - Reveals needs that partnership could address

5. **"What were you born to do?"**
   - Explores purpose and calling
   - Assesses values and mission alignment
   - Identifies vocational fit

### Operations & Resource Questions

6. **"Where is your time spent?"** (With visual graph)
   - Maps current time allocation across activities
   - Identifies priorities and constraints
   - Reveals operational focus

7. **"Biggest area for Margin increase in Time?"**
   - Identifies efficiency opportunities
   - Reveals process improvement potential
   - Assesses capacity for growth

### Final Aspirational Question

8. **"What do you want?"**
   - Open-ended question for final reflection
   - Captures desires and aspirations not covered
   - Reveals true motivations and priorities
