# VSCE, Inc. - Recruitment & Staff Augmentation Process

## Overview

VSCE uses a flexible staffing model to support client projects and internal operations. The recruitment process supports both permanent hiring and temporary staff augmentation. Recruitment is led by Armeen Haroon (Purely Works) with support from VSCE leadership and operations.

---

## Recruitment Strategy

### Staffing Model

**Permanent Hiring**
- Key positions supporting core operations
- Full-time employees with benefits
- Strategic skill requirements
- Long-term capability building

**Temporary/Contract Staff**
- Project-specific roles (engineers, managers, analysts)
- Specialized expertise on-demand
- Flexible duration (weeks to months)
- Cost-effective resource augmentation

**Staff Augmentation**
- Existing VSCE staff deployed to client projects
- Subcontractors for specialized needs
- Network of trusted partners
- Flexible resource pool

### Geographic Focus

**Northern California (Priority)**
- Bay Area (Oakland HQ focus)
- Stockton, Fresno satellites
- Local talent sourcing

**Southern California (Growth)**
- Los Angeles expansion (Arvind Joshi lead)
- San Jose (coming soon)
- Local talent recruitment

**Pacific Northwest (Strategic Expansion)**
- Washington and Oregon markets
- "Talent Ocean" initiative (Purely Works Project Three)
- Building talent pools in emerging markets

## Recruitment Process for Permanent Positions

### Phase 1: Position Definition

**Activities:**
1. Identify staffing need and business case
2. Define position requirements and qualifications
3. Determine compensation and benefits
4. Identify reporting structure
5. Develop job description
6. Approval and authorization

**Responsible Parties:**
- Regional President or department head (identify need)
- HR/Operations (job description development)
- Finance (budget authorization)
- CEO (final approval for leadership positions)

**Key Job Definition Elements:**
- Title and level
- Primary responsibilities
- Required qualifications and experience
- Desired skills and backgrounds
- Compensation range
- Benefits package
- Reporting structure
- Location and schedule

### Phase 2: Job Description Development

**Job Description Elements:**
- Position title
- Department and reporting
- Position summary (elevator pitch)
- Primary responsibilities (5-8 key areas)
- Required qualifications
  - Education (degree required/preferred)
  - Years of experience
  - Technical skills
  - Certifications
- Preferred qualifications
  - Specific industry experience
  - Software/system familiarity
  - Advanced degrees
  - Specialized expertise
- Compensation and benefits
- Application instructions

**Tone & Voice:**
- Professional yet approachable
- Clear and specific (not vague)
- Honest about demands and requirements
- Emphasize company culture and benefits
- Highlight growth and learning opportunities

**Example:**
**Program Manager - Transportation Program Management**

VSCE, Inc. seeks an experienced Program Manager to support capital program management for regional transportation agencies. Program manager will oversee multi-project coordination, stakeholder management, and strategic planning for $500M+ capital programs.

*Key Responsibilities:*
- Develop annual program schedules and budgets for multi-project programs
- Coordinate across 8-12 concurrent capital projects
- Facilitate monthly stakeholder meetings and progress reporting
- Analyze project progress and recommend optimization strategies
- Develop risk registers and mitigation plans
- Work with finance team on budget forecasting and management

*Required Qualifications:*
- Bachelor's degree in civil engineering, transportation, or related field
- 8+ years of program or project management experience
- Experience with transportation or infrastructure projects
- Strong organizational and communication skills
- Experience with project management software (MS Project or similar)
- Professional engineer license (PE) or AICP certification preferred

*Compensation:*
$120,000 - $150,000 annually
Comprehensive benefits package, flexible schedule

### Phase 3: Sourcing & Recruitment

**Sourcing Channels:**
1. **Internal Referrals** — VSCE team recommendations (often highest quality)
2. **Existing Networks** — Reach out to known candidates
3. **LinkedIn Recruiting** — Active sourcing on LinkedIn
4. **Job Boards** — Post on transportation industry boards
5. **University Networks** — Partner schools (local engineering programs)
6. **Industry Associations** — APTA, ASCE, TRB networks
7. **Recruiters** — Use specialized transportation recruiters (for hard-to-fill roles)

**Armeen Haroon's Role (Purely Works):**
- "Talent Ocean" database management (candidate pool)
- PacNW recruiting lead
- Sourcing and candidate identification
- Database and CRM coordination

**Recruitment Messaging:**
- "Join a specialized firm serving California transportation"
- "Direct access to senior expertise and leadership"
- "Meaningful work on major infrastructure projects"
- "Responsive, collaborative culture"
- "Career development in growing firm"
- "Flexible work arrangements"

### Phase 4: Candidate Screening & Selection

**Initial Screening:**
- Review resumes/applications against job requirements
- Phone screen (20-30 minutes) to assess communication and fit
- Preliminary qualification verification
- Interest confirmation

**First Interview (Phone or Video):**
- Detailed background and experience
- Technical capability assessment
- Communication and soft skills
- Cultural fit evaluation
- Compensation expectations discussion
- Interest level and timeline

**Technical Interview (if applicable):**
- Technical expertise assessment
- Problem-solving and approach
- Domain knowledge evaluation
- Reference to transportation experience

**Final Interview (In-Person if possible):**
- Meet with hiring manager and team
- Deeper cultural fit assessment
- Company presentation and vision
- Questions and concerns discussion
- Reference checking

**Selection & Offer:**
- Candidate ranking and assessment
- Reference verification
- Background check (if required)
- Offer development and negotiation
- Offer letter and acceptance

### Phase 5: Onboarding & Integration

**Pre-Arrival:**
- Equipment ordering (computer, phone, etc.)
- Office space preparation
- IT setup and access
- Documentation and paperwork
- Orientation materials

**First Day/Week:**
- Welcome and orientation
- Office tour and logistics
- IT/systems training
- Introduction to team
- Initial project/role briefing
- Cultural orientation

**First 30 Days:**
- Full project assignment and ramp-up
- Team integration and relationship building
- Client introduction (if applicable)
- Training on systems and processes
- Informal check-in and feedback
- 30-day review discussion

**First 90 Days:**
- Full productivity in role
- Client relationship established (if applicable)
- Team collaboration established
- Process and system mastery
- Formal 90-day review and feedback

## Staff Augmentation Process

### Request & Planning

**Client/Manager Request:**
- Identify specific need (role, duration, skills)
- Define deliverables and timeline
- Budget and authorization
- Project assignment

**Resource Planning:**
- Identify available internal resources
- Evaluate skill match and fit
- Determine timeline for deployment
- Discuss with resource owner (if shared)

**Sourcing Decision:**
- **Internal First:** Use existing VSCE staff if available
- **Network Second:** Reach out to trusted contractors/partners
- **Open Market:** Post and recruit if needed

### Resource Assignment

**Staff Assigned from VSCE:**
1. Discuss opportunity and timeline with resource
2. Discuss project details and expectations
3. Confirm availability and interest
4. Agree on timeline and commitment
5. Begin project transition

**Contractor/Subcontractor Assignment:**
1. Research candidate background and references
2. Confirm availability and timeline
3. Negotiate rates and terms
4. Draft Statement of Work (SOW)
5. Execute agreement and onboard

**Statement of Work (SOW) Elements:**
- Contractor name and contact
- Scope of work (specific deliverables)
- Timeline and schedule
- Rate and billing (hourly, monthly, fixed-fee)
- Deliverables and quality standards
- Communication and reporting
- Termination and changes
- Insurance and compliance requirements

### Project Execution & Management

**Initial Project Setup:**
- Project orientation and briefing
- Introduction to client/team (if applicable)
- Systems and tool access
- Schedule and expectation discussion
- Communication protocol

**Ongoing Management:**
- Weekly check-ins on progress
- Issue identification and resolution
- Schedule and budget tracking
- Client communication and coordination
- Quality assurance and oversight

**Project Completion:**
- Deliverable verification and sign-off
- Knowledge transfer (if required)
- Documentation and closeout
- Lessons learned discussion
- Invoice and payment processing
- Reference documentation (if suitable for future)

## Recruiting Metrics & Tracking

### Key Metrics

**Time-to-Hire:**
- Days from job posting to offer
- Goal: 30-45 days for standard positions
- Efficiency indicator for recruiting process

**Offer Acceptance Rate:**
- Percentage of offers accepted
- Goal: 80%+ (indicates good candidate-role fit)
- Quality of offers and candidates

**New Hire Retention:**
- Percentage still employed after 1 year
- Goal: 85%+
- Indicates good hiring and onboarding

**Quality of Hire:**
- Performance reviews and feedback
- Client satisfaction (for augmentation roles)
- Project success and contributions
- Team assessment

**Sourcing Effectiveness:**
- Percentage hired by source
- Internal referrals should be highest quality
- Network and partnership effectiveness
- Job board and recruiter ROI

### CRM/ATS Tracking

**Candidate Management System:**
- Airtable database (Project Three coordination)
- ProjectMark CRM integration
- Resume and application storage
- Interview notes and feedback
- Reference documentation
- Offer and acceptance tracking

**Reporting:**
- Monthly recruiting activity report
- Quarterly hiring summary
- Sourcing effectiveness analysis
- Time-to-hire trends
- Retention analysis

## Compensation & Hiring Guidelines

### Compensation Philosophy

**Principles:**
- Competitive with market rates for region and role
- Sustainable within business model ($247K revenue/employee average)
- Tiered by experience and expertise
- Reviewed annually and adjusted as needed

### Typical VSCE Compensation Ranges

**Junior Positions** ($60K - $80K)
- Entry-level engineer/analyst
- Recent graduate with some experience
- 0-3 years in field

**Mid-Level Positions** ($80K - $120K)
- Program manager, senior engineer
- 5-8 years experience
- Some independent project management

**Senior Positions** ($120K - $180K)
- Senior program manager, regional manager
- 10+ years experience
- Leadership and business development
- Advanced credentials

**Executive Positions** ($150K - $250K+)
- Regional president, VP roles
- 15+ years experience
- P&L responsibility
- Strategic leadership

**Contractor/Augmentation Rates:** $75 - $150+ per hour depending on expertise

### Benefits Package (Permanent Employees)

- Health insurance (medical, dental, vision)
- 401(k) retirement plan
- Paid time off (PTO) — competitive (typically 20-25 days/year)
- Professional development budget
- Flexible work arrangements
- Occasional remote work
- Industry conference attendance (supported)
- Continuing education support

## Recruiting Challenges & Solutions

### Challenge 1: Limited Talent Pool
**Specific to Transportation Consulting:**
- Relatively small market
- Geographic constraints
- Specific skill requirements

**VSCE Solutions:**
- Build relationships early (Talent Ocean approach)
- Leverage existing network and referrals
- Partner with universities and associations
- Consider partial remote work for attract talent
- Training and development of junior talent

### Challenge 2: Competitive Market (especially SoCal)
**Challenge:**
- Large firms and established competitors
- Higher compensation offers
- Brand recognition advantages

**VSCE Solutions:**
- Emphasize culture and responsiveness (not bureaucracy)
- Highlight career development and growth
- Emphasize meaningful work and impact
- Flexible arrangements
- Small firm advantages (autonomy, relationships, pace)

### Challenge 3: Retaining Key Staff
**Challenge:**
- Competition from larger firms
- Growth limitations in small firm
- Burnout risk on high-stakes projects

**VSCE Solutions:**
- Competitive compensation and benefits
- Career development opportunities
- Project variety and interesting work
- Client relationship exposure
- Remote/flexible work options
- Professional development support
- Recognition and advancement opportunities

### Challenge 4: Recruiting in Pacific Northwest
**Challenge:**
- Geographic distance from headquarters
- Unfamiliar market
- Local competitors and culture

**VSCE Solution (Project Three):**
- Long-term talent pool building (Talent Ocean)
- Relationship and trust development
- Local market research and understanding
- Partnership and referral development
- Flexible work arrangements (may not require relocation)

## Applicant Tracking & Process Management

### Using Airtable/ProjectMark CRM

**Candidate Record Fields:**
- Contact information
- Background summary
- Position applied for
- Source (how contacted)
- Phone screen completed
- Interview date and notes
- Reference information
- Offer and acceptance status
- Hire date
- Performance notes (post-hire)

**Process Workflow:**
1. Application received → Logged in ATS
2. Resume screened → Qualified or rejected
3. Phone screen → Notes and next steps
4. Interview scheduled → Calendar invites
5. Interview conducted → Notes and assessment
6. Reference check → Verification
7. Offer extended → Terms agreed
8. Hire date → Onboarding begins
9. 30/60/90 day review → Performance feedback
10. Annual review → Ongoing assessment

## Legal & Compliance

### Key Compliance Areas

**Employment Law:**
- Non-discrimination in hiring practices
- Equal Employment Opportunity (EEO) compliance
- Background check authorization and privacy
- Reference check legality

**Independent Contractor vs. Employee:**
- Classification is critical (tax and benefits implications)
- IRS 20-factor test
- Clear SOWs for contractors

**Certifications & Qualifications:**
- Verification of licenses (PE, AICP, etc.)
- Background checks for positions working with government
- Drug screening (where applicable)

**Documentation:**
- Keep hiring records and documentation
- Interview notes and assessment
- Reference check documentation
- Offer letter and acceptance
- Signed agreements (employees and contractors)
