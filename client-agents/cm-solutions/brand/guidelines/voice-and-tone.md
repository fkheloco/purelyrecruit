# CM Solutions - Voice & Tone Guide

## Overview

This guide defines how CM Solutions communicates across all channels—proposals, emails, social media, presentations, and internal documents. Our voice is consistent; our tone adapts to context.

---

## VOICE (How We Sound)

### Characteristic Words & Phrases

**CM Solutions voice is:**
- **Professional** — Polished, credible, authoritative
- **Collaborative** — Partnership-oriented, "we" language
- **Solution-Focused** — Action-oriented, outcome-driven
- **Accessible** — Clear without being condescending
- **Innovative** — Forward-thinking, modern
- **Trustworthy** — Honest, transparent, reliable

### Brand Personality

Think of CM Solutions as:
- **The Expert Consultant:** Knowledgeable, experienced, authoritative
- **The Reliable Partner:** Dependable, responsive, committed
- **The Problem-Solver:** Practical, creative, action-oriented
- **The Inclusive Colleague:** Diverse, welcoming, collaborative

### What We're NOT

- Arrogant or boastful (even when our expertise justifies it)
- Overly academic or jargon-heavy (we explain concepts clearly)
- Casual or flippant (we respect the importance of construction projects)
- Alarmist or negative (we focus on solutions, not problems)
- Generic (we customize and personalize)

## TONE (How We Adapt)

### Tone Variations by Context

#### 1. **Client Proposals (Formal, Expert, Confident)**

**Tone Characteristics:**
- Authoritative but collaborative
- Confident in expertise
- Client-focused (their success is our focus)
- Clear and well-organized
- Professional and polished

**Example Language:**
- "CM Solutions brings 20+ years of scheduling expertise to your project..."
- "Our proven methodology ensures schedule performance and cost control..."
- "We partner with you to deliver superior project outcomes..."

**What to Avoid:**
- Overly casual or conversational
- Jargon without explanation
- Vague promises ("we'll do our best")
- Negative framing ("you haven't considered...")

#### 2. **Sales & Business Development (Confident, Value-Focused)**

**Tone Characteristics:**
- Confident in differentiators
- Value-focused (what's the client benefit?)
- Forward-thinking
- Relationship-building
- Specific and concrete examples

**Example Language:**
- "As a WBE-certified firm, we provide competitive advantage for your diversity requirements..."
- "Our LOSSAN experience demonstrates our capability on complex transportation programs..."
- "Let's schedule a time to discuss how we can support your project..."

**What to Avoid:**
- Hard-sell or pushy approach
- Vague claims without backing
- Focus on CM Solutions over client benefit
- Condescending tone

#### 3. **Employee & Recruiting Communications (Warm, Inclusive, Growth-Focused)**

**Tone Characteristics:**
- Welcoming and inclusive
- Growth-oriented
- Diversity-affirming
- Collaborative
- Warm but professional

**Example Language:**
- "Join our diverse team of construction management experts..."
- "We invest in your professional development and career advancement..."
- "CM Solutions values diverse perspectives and inclusive teamwork..."

**What to Avoid:**
- Gendered language (avoid "he/she" stereotypes)
- Exclusionary language (avoid limiting experience requirements unnecessarily)
- Overly corporate or stuffy
- Hollow diversity statements (be specific and authentic)

#### 4. **Internal Communications (Clear, Collaborative, Action-Focused)**

**Tone Characteristics:**
- Direct and clear
- Collaborative
- Action-oriented
- Respectful of time
- Professional but accessible

**Example Language:**
- "Team, we have an opportunity to support a major transportation authority project..."
- "Please review the attached proposal and provide feedback by Friday EOD..."
- "Let's schedule a sync to align on the project controls approach..."

**What to Avoid:**
- Bureaucratic or overly formal
- Unclear instructions or expectations
- Blaming language
- Dismissive responses

#### 5. **Thought Leadership & Industry Communications (Expert, Authoritative, Accessible)**

**Tone Characteristics:**
- Knowledgeable and authoritative
- Clear explanation of complex topics
- Current and relevant
- Accessible to varied audiences
- Subtly confident

**Example Language:**
- "Project scheduling is more than a timeline—it's a strategic tool for cost control..."
- "Here's what we've learned from scheduling complex infrastructure projects..."
- "Construction management is evolving with AI and automation tools..."

**What to Avoid:**
- Overly academic or jargon-heavy
- Pedantic tone
- "Preaching" or talking down to audience
- Self-promotional without substance

## SPECIFIC LANGUAGE GUIDELINES

### Professional Language Standards

**Use This:**
- "CM Solutions" or "we/our" (not "the company")
- "Schedule and budget control" (not "keeping projects on track")
- "Project controls expertise" (not "what we do")
- "Deliver superior outcomes" (not "do a good job")
- "Partner with clients" (not "serve customers")
- "Proactive risk mitigation" (not "preventing problems")

**Avoid This:**
- Jargon without explanation
- Vague promises ("best-in-class," "world-class" without specifics)
- Clichés and overused phrases
- Condescending language
- Unnecessarily complex sentences
- Negative framing

### Inclusive Language Standards

**Always:**
- Use inclusive pronouns and avoid gendered assumptions
- Describe roles without gender bias (not "he/she" assumptions)
- Use "people," "team members," "professionals" instead of "guys," "folks," etc.
- Respect diverse identities and backgrounds
- Use clear, accessible language

**Avoid:**
- Gendered language (e.g., "manpower" → "staffing")
- Assumptions about experience or background
- Exclusionary language
- Stereotyping or assumptions about roles

### Examples of Inclusive Language

**Instead of:** "We need a good project manager—he should have..."
**Use:** "We're seeking an experienced Project Manager with..."

**Instead of:** "Businessmen and businesswomen in the construction industry..."
**Use:** "Professionals in the construction industry..." or "Construction industry leaders..."

**Instead of:** "Each team member brings his or her expertise..."
**Use:** "Each team member brings their expertise..."

## TONE ACROSS DIFFERENT DOCUMENTS

### Proposals

**Tone:** Formal, confident, client-focused

**Structure:**
1. **Executive Summary** — Concise, compelling, outcomes-focused
2. **Situation Analysis** — Demonstrates understanding of client needs
3. **Our Approach** — Clear, step-by-step methodology
4. **Team & Credentials** — Emphasize expertise and relevant experience
5. **Timeline & Deliverables** — Specific, realistic, measurable
6. **Why CM Solutions** — Clear differentiators, proven track record
7. **Pricing** — Transparent, value-aligned
8. **Closing** — Action-oriented, next steps

**Tone Examples:**

"Executive Summary:"
"CM Solutions brings 20+ years of construction scheduling expertise to your complex transportation program. Our proven methodology, combined with PSP-certified team members and experience on programs like LOSSAN, positions us to deliver superior schedule performance and comprehensive risk mitigation. We partner with you to ensure on-time delivery and budget control."

"Approach Section:"
"Our comprehensive project controls approach includes:
- Detailed schedule development and baseline establishment
- Ongoing schedule management and performance tracking
- Risk identification and mitigation strategies
- Executive-level reporting and stakeholder communication"

### Case Studies

**Tone:** Authoritative, results-focused, specific

**Structure:**
1. **Project Overview** — What, where, when, why
2. **Challenge** — Client's specific situation/problem
3. **CM Solutions Approach** — What we did
4. **Results/Outcomes** — Measurable impact
5. **Client Quote** — Third-party endorsement
6. **Key Learnings** — What this demonstrates about our capability

**Tone Example:**

"LOSSAN Project Case Study

**Challenge:** A major transportation authority needed expert project controls support for a complex 100+ mile commuter rail corridor improvement program involving multiple agencies, communities, and complex stakeholder coordination.

**Our Approach:** CM Solutions provided comprehensive project controls including advanced scheduling (CPM), cost management, risk assessment, and program-level coordination across multiple project phases and contractors.

**Results:**
- Maintained schedule performance despite significant stakeholder complexity
- Proactive risk management prevented costly disputes and claims
- Transparent reporting ensured stakeholder confidence and support
- Program delivered on budget and schedule

'CM Solutions was a critical partner in our project success. Their scheduling expertise and proactive approach kept our complex program moving forward.' — Transportation Authority Director"

### Job Descriptions

**Tone:** Welcoming, growth-focused, inclusive

**Structure:**
1. **Position Overview** — Role importance and impact
2. **Primary Responsibilities** — Key activities and accountabilities
3. **Required Experience** — Must-haves
4. **Desired Qualifications** — Nice-to-haves
5. **About CM Solutions** — Company culture, diversity, growth opportunities
6. **How to Apply** — Clear next steps

**Tone Example:**

"Senior Project Controls Manager

We're seeking an experienced Senior Project Controls Manager to join our growing team of construction management experts. In this role, you'll lead comprehensive project controls for complex infrastructure projects, working with experienced team members and industry-leading clients.

**What You'll Do:**
- Develop and manage project schedules using Primavera P6
- Lead cost control and budget management activities
- Identify and mitigate project risks
- Report to project executives and stakeholders
- Mentor junior team members

**About You:**
- 7+ years of project controls experience in construction
- PSP (Project Scheduling Professional) certification or PSP-eligible
- Excellent communication and stakeholder management skills
- Commitment to collaborative teamwork and diversity

**Why CM Solutions:**
CM Solutions is a women-owned, SBE/DBE-certified firm with a diverse team (40%+ women) of construction management experts. We invest in your professional development, support advancement opportunities, and value diverse perspectives and inclusive teamwork."

### Email Communications

**Tone:** Professional but warm, clear and direct, action-oriented

**Structure:**
1. **Greeting** — Personalized when possible
2. **Purpose** — Clear first sentence
3. **Body** — Key information, organized clearly
4. **Call to Action** — Clear next step or ask
5. **Closing** — Professional but warm
6. **Signature** — Complete contact information

**Tone Example:**

## DO'S & DON'TS SUMMARY

### DO

- Use "we" and "partner" language
- Focus on client outcomes and value
- Highlight relevant expertise and experience
- Reference Robyn Coates' credentials when appropriate
- Emphasize WBE/DBE certifications in relevant contexts
- Use specific examples and metrics
- Explain technical concepts clearly
- Maintain professional, polished tone
- Customize and personalize communications
- Lead with solutions, not problems
- Include clear calls to action

### DON'T

- Make it all about CM Solutions (focus on client value)
- Use jargon without explanation
- Make vague, unsupported claims
- Sound arrogant or superior
- Use gendered or exclusionary language
- Be overly casual or informal
- Make promises you can't keep
- Ignore client's specific situation
- Use generic templates without customization
- Focus on features instead of benefits
- Leave communications ambiguous about next steps

## Examples: Good vs. Not So Good

### Proposal Opening

**GOOD:**
"CM Solutions brings 20+ years of construction scheduling expertise and proven project controls methodology to your complex transportation program. Our approach combines expert-led team guidance, advanced scheduling tools, and proactive risk management to ensure schedule performance and cost control. We partner with you to deliver superior project outcomes."

**NOT SO GOOD:**
"CM Solutions is a company that provides project controls services to construction firms. We have experienced staff and can help you with your project."

### Sales Email

**GOOD:**
"Hi John, I saw that your firm was selected as prime on the new water authority project. With our water industry expertise and WBE certification, we'd be great partners for the project controls component. Could we schedule a brief call to discuss how we might support your bid?"

**NOT SO GOOD:**
"We provide project controls services and would like to work with you on your projects. Let us know if you're interested."

### Recruiting Message

**GOOD:**
"Join our diverse team of construction management experts. CM Solutions is a woman-owned firm with 40%+ women staff, committed to building an inclusive environment where diverse talent thrives. We invest in your professional development and career advancement. If you have scheduling expertise and want to work with industry-leading professionals, we'd love to talk."

**NOT SO GOOD:**
"We're hiring. Send your resume if interested."

## Voice & Tone Checklist

Before hitting send on any external communication, ask:

- [ ] Is this customized and client-focused (not generic)?
- [ ] Does it highlight relevant CM Solutions expertise?
- [ ] Is the tone appropriate for the context?
- [ ] Is the language clear and accessible?
- [ ] Are there specific examples or metrics (not vague claims)?
- [ ] Does it sound collaborative and solution-focused?
- [ ] Is it professionally polished (no typos or errors)?
- [ ] Is it inclusive and respectful?
- [ ] Does it include a clear call to action?
- [ ] Would Robyn Coates be proud to put their name on it?
