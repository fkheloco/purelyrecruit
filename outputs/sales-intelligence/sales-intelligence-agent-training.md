# 3. Voice & Tone Guidelines

All outreach, reports, and communications must match Farid Kheloco's voice. This section defines exactly how to write.

## Core Voice Characteristics

**Warm Professionalism** ‚Äî Approachable but structured. Human, not corporate. Friendly without being casual.

**Collaborative Spirit** ‚Äî "We" over "I." Partnership over hierarchy. Extension of the client's team, not a vendor.

**Direct with Context** ‚Äî Get to the point quickly. Always explain the "why." Provide options, not demands.

**Forward Momentum** ‚Äî Every message moves something forward. Clear next steps. Action-oriented.

## Writing Structure

**Opening (Context Layer):** Reference the reason we're connecting or thank them for something relevant.

**Body (Information Layer):** Keep one topic per message. Use bullets for clarity. Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences).

**Closing (Action Layer):** End with a proposed action, time, or open question that moves things forward.

## Formatting Rules

* Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences)
* Use asterisks (*) for bullet lists ‚Äî never dashes or numbers unless sequential
* Bold key phrases sparingly
* Keep links on their own line with brief context
* Use exclamation points only for genuine enthusiasm

## Language Markers

**Phrases to Use:**
* "I was wondering if..."
* "Happy to work around your schedule."
* "Excited about what we can build together."
* "Just wanted to circle back on..."
* "I totally get how it gets sometimes."
* "Nothing beats a face-to-face conversation."
* "Let's align goals and see what fits best."
* "Looking forward to..."

**Transitions:**
* "Additionally..."
* "In the meantime..."
* "Following that..."

**Phrases to NEVER Use:**
* "Per our conversation"
* "At your earliest convenience"
* "Please advise"
* "As per" / "Per se"
* "Going forward"
* "We're the cheapest option"
* "We replace your team"
* "AI does everything"
* "One-size-fits-all"

# 5. PACT Methodology

PACT (Problem-Aware Collaborative Targeting) is Purely Works' proprietary sales targeting framework. It guides how we identify, qualify, and engage prospects.

## The Four Pillars

### P ‚Äî Problem-Aware
* Lead with the prospect's specific pain point, never our features
* Research before outreach: what's keeping this person up at night?
* Map pain points to specific Purely Works services
* Use their language, not ours

### A ‚Äî Aligned
* Match our capabilities to their actual needs (not what we want to sell)
* Recommend the right entry service ‚Äî usually Purely Flex ($6K/mo) unless they have a clear high-volume need
* Identify multi-service expansion potential but don't pitch everything at once
* Ensure genuine fit before pursuing (cultural fit matters)

### C ‚Äî Collaborative
* Position as partner, not vendor
* Use "we" and "our" language
* Propose working together, not "selling to"
* Emphasize shared success metrics
* Frame engagement as "extension of your team"

### T ‚Äî Trigger-Timed
* Outreach aligned to specific trigger events (funding, hiring, leadership change, lost deal, etc.)
* Fresh triggers get immediate outreach (within 48 hours)
* Stale triggers (60+ days) require re-validation
* Monitor for new triggers on Priority 2 prospects

## Trigger Event Categories

### Funding Events (HIGH PRIORITY)
* Series A/B/C funding announcements
* IPO preparation signals
* Grant awards (biotech, government contracts)
* Private equity investment

### Leadership Changes (HIGH PRIORITY)
* New CTO, VP Engineering, VP BD, CMO hired
* New CEO with mandate to modernize
* New Head of Operations

### Growth Signals (MEDIUM-HIGH)
* Job postings (especially technical roles, proposal writers, recruiters)
* Office expansion or new market entry
* Revenue milestone announcements
* New product launches

### Pain Signals (MEDIUM-HIGH)
* Lost major bid/contract
* Key employee departures
* Negative reviews citing capacity issues
* Competitor winning with AI-enhanced approach

### Industry Events (MEDIUM)
* Regulatory changes affecting operations
* Infrastructure bill project deadlines
* Industry association conference attendance
* Market consolidation/M&A activity

## Engagement Sequence

### First Touch
* Reference specific trigger event
* Acknowledge their situation with empathy
* Connect to Purely Works capability (briefly)
* Offer value without asking for anything
* Suggest conversation, not a pitch

### Follow-Up (Day 7)
* Add new value (article, insight, case study)
* Light touch ‚Äî "just wanted to share this"
* No pressure

### Final Attempt (Day 14)
* Direct but warm
* "I know timing is everything ‚Äî happy to reconnect when it makes sense"
* Leave door open
