# Cathedral Center Ideal Customer Profile

## Primary Target: Financial Advisory Firms in Growth Mode

### Ideal Customer Definition

**Cathedral Center's ideal customer is a financial advisory firm that:**

1. **Is actively pursuing growth** through multiple vectors:
   - **Organic Growth:** Client acquisition, referral development, service expansion, team development
   - **Inorganic Growth:** M&A, advisor recruiting, platform partnerships, practice acquisitions
2. **Has capital and bandwidth** to evaluate and execute growth initiatives
3. **Values evidence-based decision-making** over gut feel and financial metrics alone
4. **Recognizes the importance of cultural fit** and integration success for sustainable growth
5. **Is willing to invest in quality evaluation** to reduce partnership failure risk and improve outcomes
6. **Has between $100M-$500M+ AUM** (primary target range, though flexible to larger or smaller)
7. **Operates independently or semi-independently** (not heavily constrained by large firm policies)

### Growth Scenarios

**Cathedral Center serves both organic and inorganic growth scenarios:**

**Organic Growth (Client & Referral Focus):**
- Identifying high-potential client prospects
- Developing referral sourcing strategies
- Building specialized service capabilities
- Expanding team capacity for growth
- Improving client experience and retention

**Inorganic Growth (Partnership Focus):**
- Recruiting advisors aligned with firm culture
- Evaluating acquisition targets for M&A
- Assessing partnership opportunities
- Integrating new advisors and firms
- Valuation and exit planning

## Firmographic Characteristics

### By Firm Size

**Small Practice (Under $100M AUM)** ⭐⭐⭐ High Fit
- Usually single advisor or small partnership
- Strong growth ambitions
- Recruiting challenges (find talent who fit)
- May be M&A acquirer or acquisition target
- Very aligned with Cathedral Center value prop

**Mid-Market Practice ($100M-$500M AUM)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ IDEAL FIT
- Established firm with growth trajectory
- Multiple advisors and team structure
- Active M&A and recruiting
- Resources to invest in quality evaluation
- Most aligned with Cathedral Center offerings

**Larger Advisory Firm ($500M+ AUM)** ⭐⭐⭐ Good Fit
- May have institutional processes
- Evaluating multiple opportunities simultaneously
- Multiple decision-makers
- Value of evidence-based assessment clear
- Some may be constrained by parent company policies

---

### By Business Model

**Independent Advisor Practices** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ IDEAL
- Full control over partnership decisions
- Direct alignment/misalignment consequences
- High pain from integration failures
- Motivated to evaluate fit
- Multiple partnership options to evaluate

**Registered Investment Advisors (RIAs)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ IDEAL
- Often independent ownership
- Growing market segment
- Active in M&A and recruiting
- Direct economics from growth
- High decision-making autonomy

**LPL Financial Affiliated Advisors** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Good Fit
- Large addressable market
- Growth through practice acquisition
- LPL ecosystem partnerships
- Some compliance constraints (acknowledge)
- Strong fit for Cathedral Center

**Wirehouse or Large Firm Affiliated** ⭐⭐ Limited Fit
- Less autonomy in partnership decisions
- Parent company may control evaluation process
- May have existing processes
- Limited growth decision authority
- Not primary target but possible secondary market

---

### By Service Model

**Wealth Management Focus** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ IDEAL
- Higher AUM practices
- More discretionary/comprehensive planning
- Emphasis on client relationships and integration
- Cultural fit very important
- Integration planning critical

**Tax-Focused or Specialized Services** ⭐⭐⭐ Good Fit
- Evaluating complementary service integration
- Culture less critical than operations
- Still benefits from assessment
- May be lower-value partnerships for Cathedral

**Team-Based Practice** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Good Fit
- Multiple advisors with interdependencies
- Cultural fit very important
- Recruiting and retention critical
- Integration challenges significant
- Strong motivation for assessment

**Solo Advisor Practice** ⭐⭐ Lower Priority
- Lower transaction value
- Individual motivations drive decisions
- Acquisition by larger firm
- Can benefit but lower priority

## Behavioral & Psychographic Profile

### Growth Orientation

**Cathedral Center Ideal Customer:**
- **Growth Ambition:** High ambition for firm growth and market expansion
- **Not Satisfied with Status Quo:** Actively seeking improvements and expansion
- **Growth Philosophy:** Believes inorganic growth (M&A, recruiting) is important
- **Strategic Thinker:** Plans beyond immediate year; thinks 3-5 year horizon

### Decision-Making Style

**Ideal Decision-Maker Profile:**
- **Data-Driven:** Values evidence and analysis, not gut feel
- **Analytical:** Wants to understand methodology and reasoning
- **Risk-Aware:** Recognizes partnership failure risk and wants to mitigate it
- **Collaborative:** Involves team in decision-making
- **Open-Minded:** Willing to consider new approaches and tools

### Values Alignment

**Shares Cathedral Center's Core Values:**
- Advisor-first orientation (serving advisor community)
- Professionalism and credibility
- Evidence and transparency
- Long-term partnership value over short-term gain
- Consultant-style guidance

## Firmographic Dimensions

### Specialty Areas

**High Fit Specialties:**
- Comprehensive Wealth Management
- Retirement Planning
- Tax Planning and Coordination
- Estate Planning Integration
- Business Owner Advisory
- Charitable Giving and Philanthropic Planning

**Moderate Fit Specialties:**
- Employee Benefit Planning
- Insurance Coordination
- Investment Management Only
- Financial Planning Only

**Lower Fit Specialties:**
- Trading-Focused Practices
- Robo-Advisor Providers
- Insurance Agent Practices
- High-Volume, Transactional Models

### Geographic Focus

**Primary Markets:**
- Major metropolitan areas (higher density of advisory firms)
- Wealth concentration areas (Bay Area, Northeast, Texas, Colorado, etc.)
- Regions with high M&A activity

**Secondary Markets:**
- Regional hubs and smaller metropolitan areas
- Areas with consolidation trends

### Age and Generational Position

**Ideal Stage:**
- **Gen X Leadership** (Age 50-65): Thinking about succession, growth, potential liquidity
- **Young Boomer Leadership** (Age 55-70): Growth still important, considering exit/transition options
- **Early Transition Advisors:** Considering succession options within next 5-10 years

**Lower Priority:**
- Very young solo practitioners (limited capital/resources)
- Near-retirement advisors (not growth-motivated)
- Succession-committed to children/staff (not M&A motivated)

## Vertical Market Segmentation

### Segment 1: Growth-Through-M&A Firms ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Profile:**
- Actively evaluating acquisition targets
- Multiple opportunities in pipeline
- Need for rapid, systematic evaluation
- Want risk reduction and integration planning
- Willing to pay premium for quality assessment

**Top Needs:**
- Due diligence and fit assessment
- Integration planning and roadmaps
- Risk identification and mitigation
- Advisor fit evaluation

**Best Positioning:**
- Evidence-based decision making
- Integration planning support
- Risk reduction and confidence
- Consultant-style guidance

**Typical Deal Size:** $5K-$25K per assessment depending on complexity

---

### Segment 2: Aggressive Recruiters ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Profile:**
- Actively recruiting advisors
- Multiple hiring needs
- High advisor turnover (problem to solve)
- Want better fit in new hires
- Willing to invest in better hiring

**Top Needs:**
- Behavioral and cultural fit assessment
- Team composition and dynamics
- Advisor retention and satisfaction
- Integration and onboarding

**Best Positioning:**
- Behavioral fit and team dynamics
- Retention and productivity improvements
- Professional advisory recruiting
- Team culture alignment

**Typical Deal Size:** Smaller per-assessment but recurring (multiple hires)

---

### Segment 3: Strategic Growth Planners ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Profile:**
- Evaluating multiple growth options
- May not know which path (M&A vs. recruiting vs. partnerships)
- Want strategic clarity and direction
- Willing to pay for strategic advisory
- Consultative relationship valuable

**Top Needs:**
- Strategic growth assessment
- Multiple option evaluation
- Market positioning and competitive analysis
- Growth roadmap development

**Best Positioning:**
- Strategic advisory and consulting
- Growth pathway clarity
- Competitive positioning
- Long-term partnership value

**Typical Deal Size:** Varies; may include ongoing advisory

---

### Segment 4: Multi-Platform/Affiliate Opportunities ⭐⭐⭐

**Profile:**
- LPL affiliated or other affiliate advisors
- Ecosystem partner of larger organization
- May be evaluating within platform partners
- Some compliance/policy constraints
- Growth motivated but within guidelines

**Top Needs:**
- Compliant evaluation process
- Integration within platform
- Advisor retention and satisfaction
- Network optimization

**Best Positioning:**
- Compliant assessment tool
- Platform-aware evaluation
- Integration planning
- Network and ecosystem value

## Decision-Making Unit (DMU)

### Primary Decision-Maker: CEO/Owner/Managing Partner
- Ultimate authority on M&A and growth decisions
- Growth and revenue responsibility
- Cultural alignment concern
- Primary buyer for strategic advisory

### Secondary Decision-Makers

**Chief Operating Officer / Operations Lead:**
- Integration planning and feasibility
- Operational compatibility
- Timeline and resource requirements
- Operational due diligence

**Chief Financial Officer / Finance Lead:**
- Financial assessment and due diligence
- ROI and investment justification
- Pricing and fee structure
- Deal economics

**Chief Compliance Officer / Compliance Lead:**
- Regulatory considerations
- Compliance history review
- Integration compliance requirements
- Risk assessment

### Influencers

**Head of Business Development / M&A Lead:**
- Deal evaluation and sourcing
- Integration planning
- Growth strategy execution
- Day-to-day assessment management

**HR / Talent Lead:**
- Team integration and culture fit
- Advisor retention concerns
- Recruiting process improvement
- Team dynamics

## Customer Journey

### First Contact Triggers

**Common situations that prompt Cathedral Center engagement:**

1. **Active M&A Process** — Firm evaluating specific acquisition target
2. **Recruiting Challenges** — Difficulty finding advisors who fit
3. **Growth Planning** — Strategic planning meeting identifies growth need
4. **Market Consolidation** — Industry consolidation trends prompt response
5. **Partner Introduction** — Referral from existing contact or partner
6. **Content Engagement** — Podcast, video, or article about advisor growth
7. **Competitive Pressure** — Competitor's growth prompts response

### Success Indicators

**Customers Most Likely to Buy & Succeed:**

- Active M&A deal in progress (not theoretical)
- Allocated budget for growth/M&A (not budget-constrained)
- Previous acquisition or growth initiative experience
- Recognized importance of cultural fit
- Open to outside advisory/consulting support

---

## Disqualifying Characteristics

**Avoid Spending Time on:**

1. **Budget Constrained** — No allocated budget for professional advisory
2. **Status Quo Focused** — Not motivated by growth; content with current size
3. **Financial-Metrics Only** — No belief in cultural fit assessment importance
4. **Highly Constrained** — Part of larger organization with all decisions made centrally
5. **Extremely Small** — Solo practitioners without resources or growth ambitions
6. **Very Specialized** — Niche practice areas without M&A activity
7. **Turnover Resistant** — No real growth ambitions despite rhetoric

---

## ICP Summary

**The Cathedral Center Ideal Customer:**

A mid-market financial advisory firm ($100M-$500M AUM) with independent decision-making authority, actively pursuing growth through M&A and/or recruiting, led by data-driven decision-makers who value evidence-based assessment and recognize the importance of cultural fit and integration success. The firm has allocated budget for professional advisory and is willing to invest in quality evaluation to reduce partnership failure risk and ensure strategic alignment.
