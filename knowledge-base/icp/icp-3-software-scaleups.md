## Business Characteristics

### Critical Pain Points
1. **AI Feature Pressure** — 77.6% of SaaS founders investing in AI features; board/investor pressure to compete
2. **Build vs. Buy Decision** — Custom development (expensive, slow) vs. API integration (loss of control, IP concerns)
3. **Engineering Capacity Bottleneck** — Hard to hire talent; competing with Big Tech; salaries inflated
4. **Technical Recruiting Difficulty** — 45-60 days to fill engineering role; high offer rejection rate
5. **Infrastructure Scaling** — Moving from early cloud to enterprise-grade (reliability, security, compliance)
6. **Speed-to-Market Pressure** — Board wants features quarterly; development cycles are long
7. **IP Ownership Concerns** — Venture-backed companies are obsessed with IP (critical for valuation); most vendors retain IP or use limiting licenses

### Current Technology Stack
* **Cloud Infrastructure:** AWS (60%), GCP (25%), Azure (15%)
* **Application Stack:** React/Vue/Next.js (frontend); Node/Python/Go/Rust (backend)
* **Data:** PostgreSQL (60%), MongoDB (30%), other specialized DBs (10%)
* **Collaboration:** Slack, Notion, Linear/Jira, GitHub
* **AI Integration:** OpenAI APIs (45%), Anthropic Claude (15%), self-hosted models (10%), evaluating others
* **Deployment:** Docker, Kubernetes, serverless (Lambda, Cloud Functions)
* **Analytics:** Mixpanel, Amplitude, custom dashboards

### Buying Triggers
* Board pressure for AI features; investor competitive concerns
* Major competitor announced AI feature; market differentiation threat
* Failed internal AI development project; timeline delays
* Key engineers leaving; can't maintain internal AI development
* Series B/C funding closes; need to accelerate roadmap
* Enterprise RFP requires AI capability; deal risk
* AWS/GCP bills exploding; cost optimization needed

### Common Objections & Counter-Arguments

| Objection | Counter |
|-----------|---------|
| "We can build this ourselves faster and cheaper" | "Your engineers bill at $150-200/hr loaded. A 3-month AI feature = $100-150K in salary alone. We deliver in 6 weeks at $60K fixed, with zero distraction to your roadmap." |
| "Open-source models are free; why pay for services?" | "Free models are commodity. Your IP, architecture, domain expertise—that's what we add. Integration, optimization, deployment, support—you're paying for execution." |
| "IP ownership; you'll use our code for other clients" | "All code, models, training data stay with you. We sign ironclad confidentiality agreements. This is our standard with funded startups." |
| "We need to retain engineering control" | "Exactly. We're your external team, not a black box vendor. Your engineers review all code; you own the repo; you maintain the feature." |
| "Cost is too high; we're managing burn" | "One quarter of engineer distraction costs more than 6 months of our services. Plus you keep engineers focused on core product roadmap." |
| "This is too specialized for outside help" | "We've worked on AI features across verticals (fintech, healthtech, edtech, martech). Patterns are similar; domain expertise you bring." |

## Entry Point & Expansion Path

### Entry Service: Fractional Dev Team (AI Features)
* **Scope:** 1-2 engineers (FTE equivalent) embedded in product team; 3-6 month projects
* **Typical Project:** AI chatbot, recommendation engine, content generation, predictive analytics, code generation
* **Engagement Model:** Weekly sprints, code review with client engineers, ownership handoff
* **Investment:** $12K-20K/month (equivalent to $150-200/hr consulting at 40 hrs/week)
* **Typical Client:** Series B/C SaaS needing AI feature but not full-time hire capacity
* **Metrics:** Feature shipped in <16 weeks, <30% rework, client engineers retained

### Alternative Entry: Fixed-Price AI Feature Project
* **Scope:** Defined AI feature (chatbot, search, generation) with fixed scope, timeline, price
* **Engagement:** 6-12 week project, client owns all code
* **Investment:** $30K-50K fixed price
* **Advantage:** Lower risk for client; easier sales conversation ("just one feature")
* **Disadvantage:** Lower margin; less recurring

### Year 1: Fractional Dev + Product Consulting
* Month 1-2: Onboarding, architecture design, team integration
* Month 3-6: Active feature development; biweekly product strategy reviews
* Month 7-12: Ongoing feature delivery; technical mentoring of internal team
* **Revenue:** $12K-18K/month
* **Deliverables:** 2-4 shipped features, technical documentation, trained internal engineers

### Year 2: Add Recruiting + Marketing Services
* Extend into technical recruiting (full-time hires) for companies experiencing growth
* AI-powered marketing features (copy generation, email personalization, content systems)
* **Revenue:** $20K-25K/month
* **Examples:** Help hire 2-3 engineers, launch AI marketing feature

### Year 3+: Add Strategic Advisory
* Product roadmap consulting (what AI features matter most?)
* Go-to-market strategy for AI capabilities
* Architecture decisions for AI at scale
* Potential equity or success-based pricing
* **Revenue:** $35K+/month
